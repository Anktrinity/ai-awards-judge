YOUR PRODUCT
Description of your technology
AI Registration Approval is a new feature within Eventpack's registration module. It adds an AI-assisted screening layer 
to any ticket type that requires organizer approval before registration is conﬁrmed. Common use cases include press 
passes, startup discounts, hosted buyer programs, VIP access, and exhibitor spots.
Setting up an approval workﬂow takes minutes. Organizers enable approval on any ticket type, select which form ﬁelds 
are relevant to the screening process, and write their criteria in plain language. No technical conﬁguration, no scoring 
rubrics to build, no external tools to connect. The criteria prompt works like a brief to a smart colleague: describe who 
should be approved and what to check, and the AI handles the rest.
From the applicant's side, the experience is straightforward. They ﬁll out the registration form as normal, with the 
addition of any approval-speciﬁc ﬁelds the organizer requires. After submitting, they receive a conﬁrmation message 
letting them know their application is under review. Once a decision is made, they are notiﬁed by email.
On the organizer's side, every incoming application is automatically analyzed by AI and presented in a dedicated 
Approval Requests dashboard. Each entry shows the applicant's AI score and status at a glance. Organizers can open 
any application to see the full submitted data alongside the AI's written analysis, then approve, reject, or reassign the 
applicant to a diﬀerent ticket type with a single click.
For events with high application volumes, organizers can conﬁgure an automatic approval threshold. Applications that 
score above the threshold are approved instantly without manual review, while lower-scoring applications remain in the 
queue for human judgment.
TECHNOLOGY DESCRIPTION
Explain how the AI works in your solution?
Eventpack's AI Registration Approval is powered by a large language model (Claude or GPT, organizer's choice). When 
an applicant submits a registration request, the AI receives two inputs: the applicant's submitted form data and the 
organizer's freeform screening criteria.
The AI actively visits any URLs submitted by the applicant, such as LinkedIn proﬁles or company websites, and veriﬁes 
that the content matches the applicant's claims. If an applicant submits a LinkedIn proﬁle that does not correspond to 
their stated identity, the AI ﬂags the inconsistency.
Against the organizer's criteria, the AI produces three outputs: a percentage score reﬂecting how well the applicant 
matches the deﬁned proﬁle, a tiered suggestion (Approve, Needs Review, or Reject), and a written explanation of its 
reasoning in plain language.
The organizer reads the AI's analysis alongside the applicant's submitted data and makes the ﬁnal decision. For high-
conﬁdence cases, organizers can conﬁgure an automatic approval threshold, allowing the AI to approve applicants 
who score above a set percentage without manual review.

Questions / Answers
What speciﬁc problem does this AI solve?
Event organizers managing gated ticket types face diﬀerent challenges depending on the nature of their event.
For large-scale conferences, the volume of applications for discounted or restricted ticket types, such as startup 
passes or academic discounts, can run into the hundreds. Reviewing each application manually is either impractical or 
requires hiring additional staﬀ solely for the validation process. Quality also suﬀers at scale: reviewers get fatigued, 
criteria get applied inconsistently, and some applicants slip through who should not.
For more boutique programs such as hosted buyer or VIP access, the problem is depth rather than volume. Each 
application requires multiple checks: verifying professional credentials, conﬁrming company details, cross-referencing 
LinkedIn proﬁles, and assessing whether the applicant genuinely ﬁts the proﬁle. This is time-consuming even for a 
small number of applicants, and the stakes of getting it wrong are higher.
In both cases, organizers are currently handling this manually with no purpose-built tooling. AI Registration Approval 
addresses both scenarios: it handles volume by processing every application instantly and consistently, and it handles 
depth by actively visiting submitted URLs, verifying identity signals, and producing a detailed written analysis for each 
applicant. Organizers spend their time making decisions, not doing research.
What makes your approach diﬀerent or innovative?
Approval workﬂows for gated registrations are not new, but the tooling for them has not kept pace. Organizers have 
largely been managing this process manually, switching between tabs, cross-checking proﬁles, and applying criteria 
inconsistently across reviewers.
AI Registration Approval addresses this by automating the veriﬁcation step, not just the data collection. The AI actively 
visits submitted URLs such as LinkedIn proﬁles and company websites, and checks whether the content matches the 
applicant's claims, rather than taking submitted information at face value.
Organizers write their criteria in plain language, the same way they would describe the ideal applicant to a colleague. 
There is no technical conﬁguration, no scoring rubrics to build, and no external tools to connect. Setup takes minutes. 
The AI applies the criteria consistently across every application regardless of volume, removing the variability that 
comes with manual review.
The system also tracks its own accuracy against the organizer's manual decisions over time, giving organizers a 
measurable basis for deciding when to switch to automated approvals or rejections. Both thresholds are fully 
conﬁgurable.

Questions / Answers
How did you test, validate, or quality-check the AI outputs to ensure they were accurate, safe, or reliable?
AI Registration Approval is currently in its pre-launch stage. Our validation approach has been built around real event 
planners and real data from the start.
We engaged with event organizers across multiple event types, including large-scale conferences, hosted buyer 
programs, and association events, throughout the design and development process. Rather than building the feature in 
isolation and testing it afterwards, we shaped the feature around their workﬂows, their pain points, and their speciﬁc 
approval criteria. Their input directly informed decisions such as the three-tier scoring system, the plain language 
criteria setup, and the auto-approval threshold conﬁguration.
For simulation testing, we worked with event planners who currently manage approval processes manually. They 
submitted real applicant data from previous events, which we used to run the AI against known outcomes. This 
allowed us to compare the AI's suggestions against decisions that had already been made by experienced humans, 
giving us a meaningful baseline for accuracy.
On the organizer side, the system is designed so that quality control does not end at launch. Before publishing an 
approval workﬂow, organizers can test their criteria against sample submissions and see how the AI responds. They 
can adjust the criteria and re-test until they are conﬁdent in the output. This is intentional: organizers know their 
audience better than any generic model, and the tool is built to reﬂect that.
The self-calibrating accuracy tracking feature, where the system monitors its own prediction rate against manual 
decisions over time, also serves as an ongoing validation mechanism built into the product itself.
IMPACT
In one summary, explain the overall impact of your AI solution. Please cover four areas: innovation, execution, user or
customer outcomes, and measurable results.
Innovation
AI Registration Approval brings genuine automation to a part of the event workﬂow that has never had it. Approval 
processes for gated ticket types have always existed, but the act of vetting each applicant has remained entirely 
manual, with no purpose-built tooling to support it.
The feature introduces active veriﬁcation rather than passive data collection. The AI visits submitted URLs and cross-
checks them against the applicant's claims, something no human reviewer can do at scale without signiﬁcant time 
investment. Combined with plain language criteria setup, a self-calibrating accuracy model, and fully conﬁgurable 
automation thresholds, it represents a meaningful shift in how organizers manage access to their events.

MODEL TRANSPARENCY (optional)
What type of AI model or technique does your solution use? Please provide a simple, high-level explanation that
helps judges understand the approach (no conﬁdential or proprietary details required).
AI Registration Approval is built on large language models (LLMs), speciﬁcally Claude by Anthropic and GPT by 
OpenAI, with organizers able to choose their preferred model. LLMs are used for their ability to interpret freeform 
natural language instructions, reason across multiple data points, and produce structured outputs alongside plain 
language explanations. No proprietary or custom model is involved.
