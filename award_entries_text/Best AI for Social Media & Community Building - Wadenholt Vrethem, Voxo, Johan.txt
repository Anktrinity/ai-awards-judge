YOUR PRODUCT
Description of your technology
Every event loses knowledge the moment the session ends. Voxo is the platform built to make sure that never happens 
again.
At its core, Voxo captures live audio from every stage simultaneously, processing each session through a proprietary 
pipeline that combines transcription, AI content generation, quality assurance, and branded publishing, all within 
minutes of a session ending. What would take an in-house production team months to deliver, Voxo produces before 
the next speaker has even left the stage.
The Intelligence Layer
Voxo is LLM-agnostic by design, integrating with OpenAI, Google Gemini, Anthropic Claude, or client-mandated 
models such as Salesforce Einstein. The platform ingests raw audio, generates structured transcripts, and applies 
multiple layers of natural language processing to extract key takeaways, speaker quotes, thematic clusters, and 
editorial-quality summaries in a single automated ﬂow.
The Product Suite
Every output is immediately branded in the event's visual identity and ready to publish. AI Session Summaries are 
delivered within minutes of each session concluding, available in desktop, mobile, and LinkedIn-optimised formats. AI 
Podcasts transform the same content into ﬁve-minute conversational audio recaps, with sponsor announcements 
woven seamlessly into the conversation. Daily and full Event Reports consolidate intelligence from every stage into 
executive-ready documents, complete with buzzword counters and deep-dive articles on trending topics. For multi-
track events, Theme Reports give each content stream its own dedicated, sponsor-ready publication.
On-site, Voxo extends beyond the screen. Digital signage surfaces real-time highlights between talks, while Live AI 
Commenting summarises sessions minute by minute and streams those insights to venue screens or attendees' 
mobile devices, ensuring no one misses a key moment regardless of which room they are in. Each speaker also walks 
away with a polished AI summary of their own session, ready to share with their networks in one click.
Human-in-the-Loop Quality Assurance
What separates Voxo from raw AI output is its dedicated QA layer. Every piece of content is reviewed by a human team 
before publishing: company and product names veriﬁed, speaker titles conﬁrmed, and speaker quotes audio-checked 
against original transcripts. The result is content that carries the editorial integrity of a professional publications team, 
produced at AI speed.
Integration, Reach, and ROI
Voxo is built to live inside existing event ecosystems rather than sit alongside them. The platform embeds directly into 
event apps via iFrame or unique session URLs, with native integrations spanning RainFocus, Swapcard, Cvent, Grip, 
ExpoPlatform, Swoogo, Amego, and more. Audio streaming piggybacks on existing AV infrastructure, making technical 
setup seamless.
All content lives in a fully branded Content Hub where every download is tracked and every share is measured. 
Organisers gain granular insight into which sessions resonated most, shaping future programming with real data. Voxo 
also opens entirely new commercial models through sponsor-branded summaries and micro-ticketing, transforming the 
post-event archive into an active revenue stream.
The result is an end-to-end AI content infrastructure that makes events immortal, sponsors measurable, and 
knowledge unstoppable.

Questions / Answers
Explain how the AI works in your solution?
Voxo captures live audio from every stage at an event simultaneously and processes each session through a 
proprietary AI pipeline that combines transcription, content generation, quality assurance, and branded publishing. The 
platform is LLM-agnostic, integrating with OpenAI, Google Gemini, Anthropic Claude, or client-mandated models such 
as Salesforce Einstein. For social media and community building, Voxo generates LinkedIn-optimised session 
summaries designed for sharing, automated LinkedIn post scheduling, and a Content Plan Generator for sustained 
post-event social activity. Each speaker receives a polished AI summary of their session, ready to share with their 
professional network in one click. The branded Content Hub serves as a community resource where attendees can 
browse, ﬁlter, and share content by stage, date, or topic, with each session getting a unique URL for easy linking and 
sharing. 5-minute AI Podcasts provide additional shareable content in audio format.
What speciﬁc problem does this AI solve?
Events generate enormous social media potential, but capturing that potential has been diﬃcult. Attendees want to 
share what they learn, but writing thoughtful posts while juggling sessions, meetings, and networking is hard. Speakers 
want to amplify their sessions but rarely have time to create shareable recaps. Marketing teams want sustained social 
activity around their event, but the content production required is resource-intensive. After the event, social buzz drops 
oﬀ quickly as people return to their daily routines. Voxo solves this by providing ready-to-share, professionally 
produced content within minutes of each session ending. LinkedIn-optimised summaries give attendees and speakers 
something compelling to post immediately. Automated LinkedIn scheduling keeps content ﬂowing after the event ends. 
The Content Plan Generator provides a structured approach to sustained social engagement. The result is that events 
become social media content engines, generating activity that extends weeks and months beyond the event dates.
What makes your approach diﬀerent or innovative?
Voxo’s social media advantage comes from turning every session and every speaker into a distribution node. Rather 
than relying solely on the event’s oﬃcial accounts, Voxo enables attendees and speakers to share content through their 
own networks. Each speaker gets a ready-to-share summary, and each attendee has access to LinkedIn-optimised 
content for the sessions they found most valuable. This decentralised distribution model multiplies reach organically. 
The automated LinkedIn scheduling and Content Plan Generator ensure sustained activity rather than a single burst of 
posts on event day. The Content Hub provides a permanent home for event content, where the community can 
continue engaging long after the event. Each session’s unique URL makes sharing speciﬁc content easy. This 
combination of ready-made social content, speaker distribution, automated scheduling, and a lasting Content Hub 
creates a social media and community-building capability that would require a dedicated content team to replicate 
manually.

Questions / Answers
How did you test, validate, or quality-check the AI outputs to ensure they were accurate, safe, or reliable?
Every piece of content Voxo produces goes through quality assurance with a dedicated human team before 
publication. For social media content, accuracy is particularly important, posts shared by speakers and attendees carry 
their personal credibility, so errors in names, titles, or key claims could be damaging.
The QA process includes: company and product names veriﬁed against a master list, speaker names, titles, and 
organisations conﬁrmed, key terminology cross-referenced with an event-speciﬁc glossary, speaker quotes audio-
veriﬁed against original transcripts, and AI-generated imagery reviewed for accuracy.
This quality-ﬁrst approach has been validated through client feedback. Steve Johnson, Global Events at HubSpot, 
described the social sharing that resulted: "It gave the event a longer life. People shared the summaries everywhere, 
and they were simple enough to take in right away." The fact that people shared content conﬁdently suggests the 
quality met their professional standards.
Richard Belcher of First Sight Media reinforced the quality point: "The accuracy of the AI summaries was truly 
impressive! As someone working with On-Demand content, I believe any way we can encourage people to consume 
content increases its value."
The validation approach has been tested at scale across The AI Summit London (176 sessions, 4,500 attendees, 
21,000+ downloads), Genesys Xperience (115 sessions, 3,300 attendees), HubSpot’s INBOUND and GROW Europe 
events, and Eventastic (15,000+ online attendees). Across all these events, content was produced reliably and at a 
quality level that supported widespread social sharing.

The Content Hub creates a permanent community resource, a searchable, ﬁlterable library of event content that people 
return to and share over time. Combined with the Content Plan Generator and automated LinkedIn scheduling, this 
creates a sustained social media presence that requires minimal manual eﬀort. The innovation is not just in content 
creation, but in creating the infrastructure for community-driven content distribution.

Questions / Answers
What type of AI model or technique does your solution use? Please provide a simple, high-level explanation that
helps judges understand the approach (no conﬁdential or proprietary details required).
Voxo’s social media and community features are powered by a multi-model AI architecture. The platform is LLM-
agnostic, integrating with OpenAI, Google Gemini, Anthropic Claude, or client-mandated models such as Salesforce 
Einstein.
The pipeline captures live audio from each stage simultaneously and converts it into structured transcripts. Natural 
language processing extracts key takeaways, speaker quotes, thematic clusters, and editorial-quality summaries. From 
this single source, the system generates multiple social-ready formats: LinkedIn-optimised summaries, desktop and 
mobile versions, podcast scripts, and contributions to daily and event reports.
For social media speciﬁcally, the system applies formatting optimised for LinkedIn’s algorithm and reading patterns: 
concise key takeaways, speaker attribution, and event branding. Speaker summaries are formatted for one-click 
sharing, reducing the friction between content creation and social distribution.
The Content Plan Generator analyses session summaries and content themes to suggest a structured post-event 
social media calendar, recommending which content to share when for sustained engagement. Automated LinkedIn 
scheduling distributes this content at planned intervals.
The Content Hub provides a permanent, branded landing page where every session has a unique URL. This supports 
social sharing through easy linking, and creates a lasting community resource that people can return to and share over 
time.
All content passes through a human QA team before publication, ensuring that what speakers and attendees share to 
their professional networks is accurate and reﬂects well on both the sharer and the event.
Integration with event platforms (RainFocus, Swapcard, Cvent, Grip, ExpoPlatform, Swoogo, and others) means 
content appears where attendees already engage, supporting community activity across multiple channels.
