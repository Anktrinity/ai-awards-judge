Explain how the AI works in your solution?
InEvent’s AI  Event Registration technology operates as an intelligent assistant that understands event registration logic 
and applies it dynamically in real time. Using language inputs, organizers can instruct the system to create, modify, or 
reorganize registration forms without manually navigating conﬁguration layers. 
The AI interprets commands to generate dynamic ﬁelds, adjust conditional logic, restructure sections, and apply 
changes instantly. It identiﬁes ﬁeld types, relationships, and dependencies to ensure updates do not break registration 
ﬂows or ticket rules. As forms evolve, the system continuously synchronizes registration data with ticketing, sessions, 
and attendee permissions. 
This allows registration inputs to automatically deﬁne onboarding paths, access rules, and session availability. The AI 
also supports contextual guidance, helping users understand how registration choices impact ticketing, session ﬂows, 
and attendee experiences. All changes are applied in real time and reﬂected immediately across mobile and web 
registration environments.
What speciﬁc problem does this AI solve?
According to the report "An Outlook on Events in 2025" by Splash, 54% of marketers track event registrations when 
measuring the success of events. Event registration is often one of the most time-consuming and error-prone parts of 
event setup. Organizers struggle with complex forms, frequent updates, inconsistent data collection, and rigid systems 
that require rebuilding ﬂows for even small changes.
InEvent’s AI  Event Registration technology solves this by removing manual conﬁguration and reducing dependence on 
technical expertise. Instead of editing multiple settings pages or recreating forms, organizers can make instant 
changes using simple instructions. 
The AI also eliminates disconnects between registration, ticketing, and onboarding. Data collected at sign-up ﬂows 
automatically into session access, permissions, and attendee setup, preventing mismatches and reducing confusion 
for participants. This results in cleaner data, faster onboarding, lower registration drop-oﬀ, and a smoother transition 
from sign-up to event participation.
What makes your approach diﬀerent or innovative?
Unlike traditional registration systems that rely on static builders, InEvent’s approach treats registration as a dynamic, 
intelligent workﬂow. The AI allows organizers to modify complex registration structures instantly without rebuilding.

How did you test, validate, or quality-check the AI outputs to ensure they were accurate, safe, or reliable?
InEvent implements a comprehensive suite of security and data privacy measures characterized by global compliance 
and robust technical safeguards, including SOC 2 Type 2, HIPAA, and PCI DSS certiﬁcations alongside full GDPR 
compliance. 
The platform utilizes world-class, fault-tolerant servers in the US and Europe with data encrypted via SSL and 
passwords protected by one-way encryption, all while adhering to NIST cybersecurity frameworks and OWASP 
security standards. 
Access is strictly managed through role-based controls (RBAC), Single Sign-On (SSO) support via SAML 2.0 and LDAP, 
and advanced monitoring for failed login attempts and malicious activity. Furthermore, InEvent ensures physical and 
operational security with redundant systems, IP range limitations, and comprehensive auditing logs, while maintaining 
high accessibility standards through WCAG 2.1 Level AA conformance. 
InEvent’s Event Registration AI is validated through a multi-layered quality assurance framework that ensures accuracy, 
reliability, and operational safety across all stages of registration and onboarding. Because registration is a mission-
critical function that directly impacts attendee access, permissions, and event ﬂow, validation is treated as a 
continuous process rather than a one-time test.
Each AI-generated or AI-modiﬁed form element is validated against predeﬁned registration rules. The system checks 
that required ﬁelds remain enforced, dependencies are preserved, and ticket or session logic is not broken by 
structural changes. Built-in safeguards prevent incomplete or conﬂicting conﬁgurations from being published, reducing 
the risk of registration errors during live campaigns.
Once forms are live, validation continues through real-time system monitoring. Every change made through the AI 
assistant, including ﬁeld edits, reordering, or logic updates, is logged and tracked. This allows organizers to review 
modiﬁcations, maintain audit visibility, and revert changes if needed. These logs also support internal quality assurance 
and compliance requirements for enterprise events.
InEvent further validates AI outputs through continuous organizer feedback. Usage patterns, correction behaviors, and 
form completion performance are analyzed to identify opportunities for reﬁnement. If users frequently adjust certain 
ﬁeld types or workﬂows, the system learns to improve clarity and guidance in future interactions. This feedback loop 
ensures the AI evolves in alignment with real operational needs rather than theoretical models.
A/B testing plays a critical role in quality assurance. Registration ﬂows are evaluated across diﬀerent conﬁgurations to 
measure completion rates, drop-oﬀ points, and user friction. These insights help validate that AI-supported structures 
not only function correctly but also improve usability and conversion.
Data integrity is a central pillar of validation. Registration inputs are continuously synchronized with ticketing systems, 
session management, and CRM integrations. Automated checks ensure attendee data remains consistent across 
platforms, preventing duplication, misalignment, and information loss during onboarding.

Execution
The InEvent’s AI Smart Registration assistant guides organizers through complex decisions, explaining ticket logic, 
session relationships, and form behavior without requiring technical expertise. This reduces dependency on support 
teams and accelerates setup timelines.
1. Enable the AI Assistant (once).
 InEvent’s AI Assistant is activated inside the platform. An event planner goes to Settings →  Tools, ﬁnds AI Assistant, 
and turns it on. Once enabled, the AI Assistant icon appears on the Registration Form page, ready to use whenever the 
form needs to be created or updated.
2. Open the Registration Form and launch the assistant.
 Inside the event, the planner navigates to the Registration Form and clicks the AI Assistant icon. This opens a chat-
style interface built speciﬁcally for registration form actions, so the planner can work in one place instead of clicking 
through multiple settings screens.
3. Create ﬁelds instantly using plain language.
 The planner describes what they need, clearly and with detail. Example:
 “Create a ﬁeld called ‘Select your country’ using an option list, and include a full list of countries.”
 The assistant creates the ﬁeld with the requested answer type and options. The same ﬂow applies to text ﬁelds, 
dropdowns, checkboxes, yes/no, and rating ﬁelds.
4. Structure the form by creating sections and moving ﬁelds.
 Next, the planner can ask the assistant to create sections (e.g., “Attendee Details,” “Preferences,” “Accessibility,” 
“Consent”) and reorganize ﬁelds:
 “Move dietary restrictions into the Preferences section and make it required.”
 This removes the usual drag-and-drop and manual restructuring eﬀort.
5.Edit live forms without breaking the experience.
 When requirements change after launch, the planner simply asks:
 “Rename ‘Company’ to ‘Organization,’ make ‘Role’ required, and reorder the top three ﬁelds.”
 Edits apply in real time, helping teams avoid version conﬂicts, duplicate forms, or last-minute rebuilds.
6.Find, clean up, and standardize ﬁelds fast.
 If the form grows complex, the assistant can ﬁnd ﬁelds by name or type, remove outdated questions, or update 
answer options across multiple ﬁelds:
 “Find all dropdown ﬁelds and update the ‘Job Function’ list to include ‘RevOps’ and ‘Partnerships.’”
7. Add conditional logic with form chains.
 To personalize onboarding, planners can ask the assistant to create or list form chains (conditional logic):
 “If attendee selects ‘VIP,’ show the dietary and accessibility questions. If ‘Press,’ show accreditation ﬁelds.”
 This ensures each attendee sees only what’s relevant, reducing friction and drop-oﬀ.

Questions / Answers
What type of AI model or technique does your solution use? Please provide a simple, high-level explanation that
helps judges understand the approach (no conﬁdential or proprietary details required).
InEvent’s Event Registration AI is built on a combination of natural language understanding, rule-based logic, and 
workﬂow automation models designed speciﬁcally for event registration and onboarding scenarios. At a high level, the 
system functions as an intelligent assistant that interprets the organizer's intent and translates it into structured 
registration logic. When an organizer provides an instruction, such as creating a new ﬁeld, updating options, 
reorganizing sections, or modifying requirements, the AI ﬁrst applies natural language processing (NLP) to understand 
the requested action and which registration elements are involved. Once intent is identiﬁed, the system applies event-
speciﬁc rule logic to safely execute the change. This logic layer understands how registration forms are structured, 
including ﬁeld types, dependencies, required settings, conditional logic, and relationships with tickets, sessions, and 
attendee permissions. Rather than generating content freely, the AI operates within predeﬁned frameworks that ensure 
accuracy and consistency. For example, when creating dynamic ﬁelds, the system selects appropriate ﬁeld types such 
as text inputs, dropdowns, checkboxes, rating scales, or yes/no questions based on the instruction. When modifying 
existing ﬁelds, the AI identiﬁes them by name or type and applies updates without breaking dependencies or data 
validation rules.
Real-time editing is supported through continuous state awareness. The AI maintains an up-to-date understanding of 
the current registration structure, allowing it to reorder sections, move ﬁelds, or update values instantly while 
preserving form integrity. This enables organizers to make live adjustments without rebuilding registration ﬂows.
Styling features, such as subtitles or ﬁeld descriptions, are generated using language models trained to adapt tone and 
formatting while remaining within brand-safe parameters deﬁned by the organizer. These outputs enhance clarity and 
presentation without aﬀecting registration logic. Beyond form building, the AI also provides contextual guidance. It 
draws from structured platform knowledge to explain how ticketing, sessions, and attendee ﬂows work together. This 
guidance layer helps organizers understand cause-and-eﬀect relationships within the registration and onboarding 
process, particularly useful for new team members or complex event setups. Importantly, the AI does not operate 
autonomously. All actions occur within clearly deﬁned event boundaries and require organizer initiation. The system 
does not create, modify, or distribute attendee data independently. It functions as a controlled assistant that supports 
decision-making rather than replacing it. From a transparency standpoint, every change triggered by the AI is visible to 
the organizer, logged within the platform, and reversible. This ensures auditability and maintains trust, particularly for 
enterprise environments where registration accuracy is critical. By combining natural language understanding with 
structured event logic and controlled automation, InEvent’s Event Registration AI delivers intelligent assistance that 
feels intuitive while remaining predictable, safe, and reliable enabling faster setup, cleaner data, and smoother 
onboarding without sacriﬁcing control.
