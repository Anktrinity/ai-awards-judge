Description of your technology
eBrain is not an attendee management tool with AI features. eBrain is an AI-centric operating model that redeﬁnes the 
entire logic of how attendees are registered, managed, communicated with, checked in, and personally guided through 
their event experience.
The event industry has digitized attendee management - but it has not made it intelligent. Behind every successful 
event lies an invisible layer of rigid registration forms, fragmented participant data, manual change cascades, 
disconnected check-in systems, and generic mass communication. Attendee management teams spend the majority 
of their working hours not on guest experience, but on data reconciliation and coordination overhead. eBrain was built 
to solve this structural problem.
Five-layer architecture for attendee management excellence:
eBrain organizes the complete attendee journey across ﬁve interconnected layers: The Experience Layer delivers AI-
generated event websites, intelligent registration with shopping cart, add-ons, group logic, ﬂexible ticketing, QR-based 
check-in, and a personalized mobile app that extends the attendee experience from screen to venue - with 
individualized agendas, real-time updates, and contextual event information. The Operations Layer manages 
participant data, hotel contingents, session assignments, transfer logistics, catering preferences, surveys, and change 
management - all synchronized in real time. The Intelligence Layer embeds AI into every attendee touchpoint: 
registration optimization, hyperpersonalized communication by participant type, real-time contingent steering, and 
Emily as the dialogic access and action layer - powered by an agentic architecture where the LLM reasons, selects 
tools, and chains actions autonomously. The Synchronization Layer serves as the single source of truth, keeping 
attendee data, statuses, and contingent allocations consistent across all touchpoints - eliminating the manual 
reconciliation cascades that cause data inaccuracies. The Trust & Compliance Layer ensures EU-sovereign hosting, 
GDPR compliance, and EU AI Act readiness — critical for handling sensitive attendee data.
Four core engines across the attendee lifecycle:
The Setup Machine enables AI-powered event website creation via wizard and prompting, registration conﬁguration 
with conditional logic, ticketing setup, and campaign launch. The Run Machine delivers real-time attendee monitoring, 
automated and personalized communication workﬂows, synchronized change management, and live contingent 
steering for hotels, sessions, shuttles, and catering. The Onsite Machine handles branded badge generation with QR 
codes, sub-30-second check-in, and live attendance status - eliminating queues and manual lists. The Learn Machine 
captures registration funnel analytics, check-in throughput metrics, NLP-processed attendee feedback, and historical 
benchmarks for continuous improvement.
Emily -  the attendee management intelligence layer:
Emily is not a chatbot feature. Emily is a dialogic access, guidance, and action layer for the entire attendee 
management operation. Event managers ask natural-language questions (“How many VIPs have completed 
registration?”, “Which hotel contingent is overbooked?”), receive immediate answers with action recommendations, 
and can trigger actions directly - such as sending a personalized reminder to incomplete sign-ups. Emily makes even 
less experienced team members immediately eﬀective and dramatically reduces staﬀ onboarding time.
The result: an attendee management experience where participants move from personalized invitation through intuitive 
registration and mobile event companion to frictionless badge scan - and event teams move from data chaos to 
intelligent orchestration.

Explain how the AI works in your solution?
AI operates in eBrain’s attendee management stack across three clearly diﬀerentiated levels - not as a single feature, 
but as a continuous intelligence layer spanning the entire attendee journey from registration to post-event learning.
LLM level: High-performance language models generate event website and registration page content, optimize 
communication copy, process natural-language queries about attendee status, and deliver contextual assistance. A 
corporate design crawler automatically extracts brand identity to ensure every attendee touchpoint matches the 
client’s visual identity.
Assistant level: Specialized AI assistants guide users through registration setup, personalized attendee communication, 
contingent management, and check-in conﬁguration. They deliver contextual guidance and action recommendations 
adapted to the current event status and user role
Agent level: Task-oriented AI agents handle concrete attendee operations - the CTA agent ﬂags incomplete 
registrations and overbooked contingents, while other agents manage conﬁrmation workﬂows, data reconciliation, and 
cross-touchpoint status updates when attendees modify their bookings.
Emily uniﬁes all three levels as the dialogic interface: teams interact through natural language, receiving real-time 
answers about registration status, contingent availability, and attendee data - without context switches. The entire AI 
infrastructure runs on EU-sovereign cloud (IONOS), is GDPR-compliant by design, and addresses EU AI Act 
requirements.
What speciﬁc problem does this AI solve?
Event attendee management does not have a digitization problem. It has an intelligence problem.
Existing tools have moved registration online - but they have not made the attendee journey intelligent. They replicate 
rigid, form-based workﬂows on a digital surface: disconnected data silos between registration, communication, 
logistics, and check-in. Generic mass emails instead of personalized attendee communication. No real-time contingent 
visibility. Check-in systems that create queues instead of eliminating them. No connected mobile experience that 
extends personalization to the venue. The result is legacy thinking running on modern infrastructure - consuming 
capacity without improving the attendee experience or data quality.
A single attendee change - a cancellation, a room switch, a dietary update - triggers manual follow-up across ﬁve or 
more touchpoints: update the participant list, notify the hotel, adjust the shuttle plan, correct the badge, revise the 
catering count, adapt the conﬁrmation. These cascades cost time, produce data inconsistencies, and degrade user 
experience.
eBrain solves this by rebuilding the entire attendee management logic as AI-centric - synchronized, dialogic, 
personalized, and designed for zero-friction attendee journeys from ﬁrst invitation to post-event feedback.

How did you test, validate, or quality-check the AI outputs to ensure they were accurate, safe, or reliable?
eBrain’s validation strategy for attendee management follows one principle: data accuracy and user experience before 
technological ambition. Every AI application is measured against real operational outcomes - queue times, data 
consistency, and attendee satisfaction.
1. Foundation from 20+ years of attendee management validation
The underlying attendee management logic is not theoretical - it has been tested in real event projects across 
industries and complexity levels for over two decades. This operational experience deﬁnes where AI creates genuine 
leverage in the attendee journey and where human control remains essential. eBrain was not built from technology 
enthusiasm, but from deep understanding of real attendee management bottlenecks: form abandonment, data 
inconsistencies, check-in delays, communication fragmentation, and onboarding friction.
2. Surgical AI integration by attendee management use case
Rather than deploying AI generically, the most critical bottlenecks were analyzed individually: registration page 
creation, data validation, change synchronization, personalized communication, contingent management, badge 
generation, check-in throughput, and mobile experience delivery. For each use case, the team deﬁned which AI 
intervention serves a clear purpose and which quality parameters apply. This surgical approach ensures AI is only 
deployed where it measurably improves speed, data accuracy, or user experience.
3. Three-level quality assurance for attendee data
At the LLM level, generated content - registration pages, conﬁrmation messages, event website copy - is constrained 
by contextual parameters and brand logic. The corporate design crawler ensures visual consistency across all 
attendee-facing touchpoints. At the assistant level, recommendations are bound to actual attendee data - assistants 
work on real participant, contingent, and status information, not hallucinated data. At the agent level, triggered actions 
such as sending conﬁrmations or updating contingents are presented as suggestions - ﬁnal approval remains with the 
human operator. This responsible AI design ensures data accuracy is never compromised by automation.
4. Synchronization Layer as data accuracy guarantee
The Synchronization Layer simultaneously functions as a validation layer: attendee changes are not processed in 
isolation but reconciled system-wide. Inconsistencies between touchpoints - such as divergent participant statuses in 
badge systems, hotel contingent lists, and the mobile app - are detected and resolved before they produce check-in 
errors or data discrepancies. This architectural approach to data integrity is what separates eBrain from systems that 
merely digitize manual reconciliation.
5. EU-sovereign infrastructure as trust foundation
Attendee data is among the most sensitive in event operations - personal information, dietary requirements, 
accessibility needs. The entire platform runs on IONOS EU cloud with GDPR-compliant data processing. The 
architecture addresses EU AI Act requirements regarding risk assessment and documentation. Data sovereignty and 
traceability are architecturally embedded - not as a retrospective compliance exercise, but as an integral part of system 
design.
6. Continuous optimization through attendee analytics
KPI measurements for check-in throughput, registration completion rates, data accuracy, and attendee satisfaction are 
collected on a project-speciﬁc basis and feed into continuous improvement of AI models, assistants, and agents. The 
Learn Machine closes the feedback loop: attendee insights from every event improve quality for the next.

Questions / Answers
What type of AI model or technique does your solution use? Please provide a simple, high-level explanation that
helps judges understand the approach (no conﬁdential or proprietary details required).
eBrain employs a multi-layer AI architecture connecting three technology levels — each optimized for diﬀerent 
operational requirements in attendee management.
Foundation models and provider architecture:
The primary LLM is GPT-4.1, accessed via Azure OpenAI, delivering high-performance reasoning for content 
generation, natural-language query processing, and agentic tool orchestration. For resilience and EU data sovereignty, 
an automatic fallback routes to Meta Llama 3.3-70B-Instruct hosted on IONOS EU cloud - ensuring uninterrupted 
operation even when primary provider content ﬁlters trigger on edge-case inputs. Image generation uses Black Forest 
Labs FLUX.1-schnell, also hosted on IONOS, for AI-powered event visuals and registration page graphics. This dual-
provider architecture guarantees both performance and EU-sovereign data processing.
Agentic architecture with function calling:
Emily operates through an agentic loop - the LLM reasons about user intent, selects from tool functions across both 
the Python AI layer and the Ruby backend, executes them, evaluates results, and chains further actions - up to ten 
iterations per interaction. Safe operations auto-execute via whitelisting; state-changing actions require explicit user 
conﬁrmation.
Specialized AI assistants:
Along every phase of the attendee lifecycle - Setup, Run, Onsite, Learn - contextually embedded assistants operate. 
These are conﬁgured for speciﬁc attendee management tasks: registration setup assistance, communication 
personalization, contingent management, check-in conﬁguration, and analytics interpretation. They deliver contextual 
guidance adapted to the current event status and user role.
Task-oriented AI agents:
Agents take over or support concrete attendee management operations. The CTA agent ﬂags incomplete registrations, 
overbooked contingents, and pending conﬁrmations. Additional agents support communication creation, data 
reconciliation, status updates, and action recommendations. Agents operate in a suggestive mode - ﬁnal decisions 
remain with the human operator.
NLP for attendee feedback analysis:
Natural Language Processing is deployed in the Learn Machine to analyze free-text feedback about the registration, 
check-in, and overall attendee experience - pattern recognition, sentiment analysis, and automated summaries enable 
insights that would take days manually.
Emily as the integrating dialog layer:
Emily is the user-facing interface connecting all three AI levels. She processes natural-language queries about attendee 
status, routes them to the appropriate subsystem, and delivers answers, recommendations, or action suggestions in a 
uniﬁed dialogic interaction.
Technical stack and infrastructure:
Python FastAPI microservices for AI processing, Rails 8 backend for data persistence and tool execution, Nuxt 3 and 
Vue frontend, PostgreSQL for operational and AI session data. YAML-based prompt engineering with versioned 
templates ensures reproducible AI behavior. The entire architecture runs on EU-sovereign cloud infrastructure (IONOS), 
is GDPR-compliant, and addresses EU AI Act documentation requirements.
