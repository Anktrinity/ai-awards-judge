Description of your technology
MyGraduationClip is an AI-powered platform designed to transform full-length graduation recordings into personalised, 
shareable video content at scale, delivered within minutes of the ceremony.
The platform collects video from any source, including live streams, in-house media teams, or third-party production 
partners. This ﬂexibility is essential in the graduation environment, where setups vary signiﬁcantly between institutions. 
Whether working with single-camera footage or complex multi-camera productions, the platform intelligently adapts to 
the input without requiring a ﬁxed ﬁlming conﬁguration or manual re-engineering.
Once ingested, the system processes the footage using a combination of audio analysis, computer vision, and 
temporal pattern recognition. It identiﬁes key moments within the ceremony (such as when each graduate is 
announced and crosses the stage) and segments the content into individualised clips. This replaces what was 
traditionally a highly manual and time-intensive editing process, enabling thousands of clips to be generated rapidly 
and consistently.
A key strength of the platform is its ability to operate reliably in unpredictable live-event conditions. Graduation 
ceremonies often involve variable lighting, inconsistent audio quality, and diﬀering camera angles. The system is 
designed to make intelligent decisions in real time, accounting for these uncertainties and maintaining accuracy 
without requiring controlled environments, meaning it can reliably process virtually any footage provided.
The platform also incorporates a conﬁdence-based matching and validation layer. Where certainty is high, clips are 
automatically delivered. If there’s any uncertainty,, graduates are presented with a small number of thumbnail options 
to conﬁrm their identity. This ensures both speed and accuracy, enabling the system to scale while maintaining a high-
quality user experience.
Once generated, clips are placed into a pre-agreed template containing university branding, messaging, and calls to 
action (such as encouraging graduates to join the alumni community). These personalised videos are delivered 
instantly via a mobile-ﬁrst interface, optimised for social sharing. This immediacy is central to the platform’s impact, 
allowing graduates to share their moment while it is still fresh, signiﬁcantly increasing engagement, reach and graduate 
satisfaction.
Beyond content delivery, the platform includes integrated data capture and analytics capabilities. By embedding 
lightweight surveys into the user journey and analysing engagement patterns, it provides universities with actionable 
insights to support alumni engagement, marketing strategies, and long-term community development.
In summary, MyGraduationClip combines adaptable data intake, intelligent media processing, and scalable delivery 
into a single platform. We use AI to turn complex live event footage into meaningful, personalised experiences that 
drive both real-time social engagement and lasting community connection.

Questions / Answers
Explain how the AI works in your solution?
Our AI combines audio analysis, computer vision, temporal pattern matching, and conﬁdence-based validation to 
transform full-length graduation recordings into personalised clips at scale.
1. Audio Segmentation (ASR)
The system uses automatic speech recognition to analyse ceremony audio and identify likely name-announcement 
moments. As audio can be noisy and names vary in pronunciation, we do not rely on a ﬁxed name list. Instead, each 
registered graduate’s name is matched to likely segments using text-similarity techniques.
2. Visual Detection (Computer Vision)
In parallel, the system analyses video to track how individuals enter the frame, cross the stage, and exit, while also 
identifying camera angle changes. As ceremonies follow consistent patterns, this enables detection of likely “graduate 
moment” sequences.
3. Matching & Validation
The most likely audio and video segments are aligned to generate a clip, with matches ranked by conﬁdence. Where 
needed, graduates conﬁrm their identity via a small set of thumbnails, ensuring accuracy without slowing delivery.
4. Clip Generation
Validated footage is placed into a pre-built template with branding, messaging, and optional calls to action, creating a 
personalised, shareable video. This process enables fast, reliable clip generation at scale.
What speciﬁc problem does this AI solve?
Graduation is one of the most emotional and shareable moments in a person’s life. But historically, it hasn’t been 
delivered in a way that supports real-time social sharing or sustained community engagement.
Traditionally, graduates either had to watch full-length ceremony recordings (often over 90 minutes) to ﬁnd their 
moment (as little as 8 seconds) or wait weeks for manually edited clips. By the time content was available, the peak 
moment for sharing had passed, signiﬁcantly limiting social reach and engagement.
Our AI-powered solution removes this barrier entirely. Each graduate receives a personalised, high-quality clip within 
minutes transforming a process that historically required signiﬁcant delay and manual editing - making it instantly ready 
to share across social platforms while the moment is still fresh. This drives signiﬁcantly higher levels of organic sharing, 
amplifying both personal celebration and institutional visibility.
Beyond social, we address a second critical challenge: maintaining connection with graduates after the ceremony. By 
integrating a simple, high-engagement survey into the clip delivery journey, we capture valuable insight at the point of 
peak interaction. This has transformed alumni engagement, increasing sign-ups from an average of 6% to 78%, 
turning a ﬂeeting moment into the foundation of a long-term community.

Questions / Answers
How did you test, validate, or quality-check the AI outputs to ensure they were accurate, safe, or reliable?
Reliability has been a core design principle of our AI system from the outset, as graduation is a once-only, emotionally 
signiﬁcant event. Our quality assurance approach combines automated validation, conﬁdence-based user veriﬁcation, 
continuous learning, and robust exception handling.
1. Automated Validation Checks
Outputs are validated at multiple stages of the pipeline. These include:
•  Clip-duration checks, 
• Audio-presence veriﬁcation, 
• Duplicate-segment detection, and conﬁdence-threshold checks to ﬂag uncertain matches before delivery. 
• Where possible, person-presence checks are also applied to ensure clips are visually plausible and aligned with 
the intended moment.
2. Conﬁdence-Based User Validation
Rather than assuming the AI is always correct, the system ranks potential matches by conﬁdence. Where certainty is 
lower, graduates are presented with a small set of thumbnail options (typically up to six), ordered by likelihood. Users 
can conﬁrm their identity or select “none of these are me.” This human-in-the-loop approach ensures accuracy while 
maintaining speed, and acts as a key real-world quality control layer.
3. Continuous Learning from User Behaviour
We analyse user selections and correction patterns from the validation process to reﬁne matching logic over time. This 
creates an ongoing feedback loop, allowing the system to adapt to authentic variation in ceremony formats, audio 
quality, and naming complexity.
4. Exception Handling for Everyday Variability
Graduation footage varies signiﬁcantly depending on ﬁlming setup, lighting, audio quality, and transmission conditions. 
Where footage is incomplete or degraded, the system attempts to recover usable clips wherever possible. If this is not 
viable, cases are routed into an exception process (for example, requesting replacement footage). Dedicated exception 
queues ensure issues are identiﬁed and resolved, rather than producing poor outputs.
5. Validation at Real-World Scale
The platform operates in live graduation environments, processing large volumes of ceremonies concurrently, with up 
to 700 clips generated in a single event. This means quality assurance is embedded in usage, not just controlled 
testing. We also conduct manual spot checks on selected ceremonies, particularly when reviewing new scenarios or 
unusual conditions.
Overall, our approach is simple: automate where conﬁdence is high, validate where ambiguity exists, and manage 
exceptions where inputs are imperfect. This ensures the system remains accurate, reliable, and dependable at scale.

Describe how the AI was used in practice.
MyGraduationClip is designed to integrate seamlessly into the graduation journey, using AI at multiple stages to 
transform raw ceremony footage into instantly shareable, personalised content at scale.
Before the ceremony
Graduate data is securely collected and prepared within the system. This includes name information, which is used to 
support accurate identiﬁcation during processing. The platform is conﬁgured to align with each institution’s ceremony 
structure, ensuring a smooth and consistent experience on the day.
During the ceremony
Video and audio feeds are captured in real time as graduates cross the stage. These feeds are ingested directly into the 
platform, ready for immediate processing.
AI processing and clip creation
The system separates the audio and video streams. The audio is analysed and segmented into individual name 
announcements, identifying the precise moment each graduate is presented. In parallel, the video is analysed to detect 
key visual markers - such as when a graduate steps onto the stage, crosses it, and exits - across multiple camera 
angles.
These elements are then intelligently recombined, matching the correct audio announcement to the corresponding 
video segment to generate a clean, personalised clip for each graduate. This replaces what was traditionally a manual 
editing process, enabling accurate clip creation at scale and in near real time.
Validation and delivery
To ensure accuracy, an AI-assisted validation layer is built into the user journey. Graduates are presented with one or 
more thumbnail images based on system conﬁdence and simply conﬁrm their identity. Once validated, the 
personalised clip is instantly delivered via a mobile-ﬁrst platform, optimised for social sharing. Because this happens 
within minutes (often before the graduate leaves the venue) the content is shared at the peak moment of excitement, 
signiﬁcantly increasing engagement and reach.
Post-event engagement and insight
Alongside delivery, the platform introduces a short, targeted survey, capturing valuable insight at the point of highest 
engagement. This data is analysed within the platform to generate actionable insights, helping universities better 
understand and grow their graduate communities.
In practice, this creates a seamless, end-to-end system where AI transforms complex live event data into meaningful, 
shareable moments - driving both immediate social engagement and long-term community connection.

Questions / Answers
What type of AI model or technique does your solution use? Please provide a simple, high-level explanation that
helps judges understand the approach (no conﬁdential or proprietary details required).
MyGraduationClip uses a hybrid, multimodal AI approach that combines several techniques to process complex live-
event footage and generate personalised clips at scale.
1. Audio Analysis (ASR + Similarity Matching)
We use automatic speech recognition (ASR) to analyse ceremony audio and identify likely name-announcement 
segments. As live audio can be noisy and names are often pronounced diﬀerently, we do not rely on exact matches. 
Instead, we apply similarity-based techniques (including trigram and TF-IDF comparisons) to align registered names 
with detected speech segments.
2. Computer Vision (Video Understanding)
In parallel, computer vision techniques analyse the video feed to detect patterns such as individuals entering and 
leaving the frame, movement across the stage, and changes in camera angle. These signals allow the system to 
identify likely “graduate moments” without requiring predeﬁned zones or ﬁxed ﬁlming setups.
3. Temporal Clustering & Sequence Reconstruction
Graduation footage often includes multiple camera angles within a single feed. Using temporal clustering and pattern 
analysis, the system groups related shots based on timing and camera behaviour, reconstructing a coherent sequence 
for each individual.
4. Conﬁdence Scoring & Matching
The system applies conﬁdence scoring to determine the best alignment between audio and video segments. This 
enables prioritisation of high-conﬁdence matches while ﬂagging ambiguous cases for further validation.
5. Human-in-the-Loop Validation
Where conﬁdence is lower, users conﬁrm their identity through ranked thumbnail selection. This ensures accuracy 
while maintaining scalability and speed.
In addition, clustering techniques based on multi-dimensional embeddings are used to improve shot grouping and 
segmentation. While large language models (LLMs) are not used in clip generation, they are used elsewhere in the 
platform for tasks such as survey moderation and analytics.
Overall, the platform operates as a hybrid AI workﬂow (combining speech recognition, computer vision, similarity 
matching, temporal clustering, and validation) to reliably transform complex, real-world event data into personalised, 
shareable content at scale.
