Description of your technology
The combined solution developed by MyGraduationClip and First Sight Media integrates AI-powered content 
processing with industry-leading live event production workﬂows to enable real-time, personalised video delivery at 
scale.
At its core, MyGraduationClip provides a multimodal AI platform that transforms full-length graduation recordings into 
individualised, shareable clips. Using a combination of audio analysis, computer vision, and temporal pattern 
recognition, the platform identiﬁes key moments within the ceremony - such as when each graduate is announced and 
crosses the stage - and segments the footage into personalised videos. This replaces what was traditionally a manual 
editing process, enabling hundreds or thousands of clips to be generated rapidly and consistently.
However, the eﬀectiveness of this AI is directly dependent on the quality, structure, and speed of the input footage. 
This is where First Sight Media’s role is crucial.
As the UK’s leading provider of graduation video production, streaming, and AV solutions, First Sight Media delivers 
high-quality, multi-camera footage through a production workﬂow speciﬁcally optimised for rapid processing and 
upload to MyGraduationClip. Through close collaboration, this process has been reﬁned to ensure that footage is 
captured, prepared, and transferred with minimal delay immediately following each ceremony (more speciﬁcally, 
immediately after the last graduate has crossed the stage).
This integration removes one of the biggest bottlenecks in traditional methods: the delay between ﬁlming and 
processing. Where most solutions rely on delayed uploads or post-event handling, the combined approach enables 
footage to move seamlessly from production into AI processing within minutes.
The platform is also designed to operate in real-world conditions, handling variability in camera setups, lighting, and 
audio quality without requiring a ﬁxed ﬁlming conﬁguration. This ﬂexibility allows the solution to be deployed 
consistently across a wide range of institutions and ceremony formats.
Once processed, clips are placed into pre-agreed branded templates and delivered instantly via a mobile-ﬁrst platform, 
optimised for social sharing. The speed of this end-to-end system means that, in many cases, graduates receive their 
personalised clips before the ceremony has even concluded.
In summary, this collaboration combines AI-driven media processing with production-grade workﬂow design. By 
creating a fully integrated system we’ve transformed complex live event footage into real-time, shareable content, and 
signiﬁcantly redeﬁnes what is possible in graduation delivery.

Questions / Answers
Explain how the AI works in your solution?
Our AI combines audio analysis, computer vision, temporal pattern matching, and conﬁdence-based validation to 
transform full-length graduation recordings into personalised clips at scale.
1. Audio Segmentation (ASR)
Automatic speech recognition analyses ceremony audio and identiﬁes likely name-announcement moments. As audio 
can be noisy and names vary in pronunciation, we do not rely on a ﬁxed name list. Instead, each registered graduate’s 
name is matched to likely segments using text-similarity techniques.
2. Visual Detection (Computer Vision)
In parallel, the system analyses video to track how individuals enter the frame, cross the stage, and exit, while also 
identifying camera angle changes. As ceremonies follow consistent patterns, this enables detection of likely “graduate 
moment” sequences.
3. Matching & Validation
The most likely audio and video segments are aligned to generate a clip, with matches ranked by conﬁdence. Where 
needed, graduates conﬁrm their identity via a small set of thumbnails, ensuring accuracy without slowing delivery.
4. Clip Generation
Validated footage is placed into a pre-built template with branding, messaging, and optional calls to action, creating a 
personalised, shareable video.
Within the collaboration, First Sight Media’s production workﬂow ensures that high-quality footage is delivered rapidly 
into the AI system - enabling fast, reliable clip generation.

Questions / Answers
How did you test, validate, or quality-check the AI outputs to ensure they were accurate, safe, or reliable?
Reliability has been a core design principle of our AI system from the outset, as graduation is a once-only, emotionally 
signiﬁcant event. Within this collaboration, quality assurance is achieved through a combination of:
• automated validation, 
• conﬁdence-based user veriﬁcation, 
• continuous learning, 
• and robust exception handling
All of which is supported by a production workﬂow designed to deliver consistent, high-quality input.
1. Automated Validation Checks
Outputs are validated at multiple stages of the pipeline. These include clip-duration checks, audio-presence 
veriﬁcation, duplicate-segment detection, and conﬁdence-threshold checks to ﬂag uncertain matches before delivery. 
Where possible, person-presence checks are also applied to ensure clips are visually plausible and aligned with the 
intended moment.
2. Conﬁdence-Based User Validation
Rather than assuming the AI is always correct, the system ranks potential matches by conﬁdence. Where certainty is 
lower, graduates are presented with a small set of thumbnail options, ordered by likelihood. Users can conﬁrm their 
identity or select “none of these are me.” This human-in-the-loop approach ensures accuracy while maintaining speed 
and acts as a key real-world quality control layer.
3. Continuous Learning from Real-World Use
We analyse user selections and correction patterns from the validation process to reﬁne matching logic over time. This 
creates an ongoing feedback loop, allowing the system to adapt to real-world variation in ceremony formats, audio 
quality, and naming complexity.
4. Exception Handling for Live Event Variability
Even with a highly optimised production workﬂow, live events remain inherently unpredictable. Variations in venue 
conditions (such as lighting, audio environments, or infrastructure) can still impact footage quality. The system is 
designed to account for these factors, recovering usable clips wherever possible. Where issues cannot be resolved 
automatically, cases are routed into a managed exception process to ensure no graduate is missed.
5. Quality Through Workﬂow Integration
A key strength of the collaboration is the consistency of input provided by First Sight Media’s production workﬂow. By 
capturing high-quality, multi-camera footage and transferring it rapidly after each ceremony, the system beneﬁts from 
reliable, well-structured input—reducing ambiguity and improving overall accuracy.
6. Validation at Real-World Scale
The platform operates in live graduation environments, processing large volumes of ceremonies concurrently, with up 
to 700 clips generated in a single event. This ensures quality assurance is embedded in real-world usage, not just 
controlled testing. We also conduct manual spot checks on selected ceremonies, particularly when reviewing new 
scenarios or unusual conditions.
Overall, our approach is simple: automate where conﬁdence is high, validate where ambiguity exists, and manage 
exceptions where inputs are imperfect. Within this collaboration, the combination of high-quality production and AI-
driven validation ensures the system remains accurate, reliable, and dependable at scale.

Describe how the AI was used in practice.
MyGraduationClip and First Sight Media work together to integrate AI seamlessly into the live graduation experience, 
transforming raw ceremony footage into personalised, shareable content within minutes.
Before the ceremony
Graduate data is securely collected and prepared within the system. This includes name information, which supports 
accurate identiﬁcation during processing. The platform is conﬁgured to align with each institution’s ceremony structure, 
ensuring a smooth and consistent experience on the day.
During the ceremony
First Sight Media captures high-quality video and audio feeds as graduates cross the stage. This production workﬂow 
is designed to ensure footage is clean, consistent, and ready for rapid transfer immediately after each ceremony 
segment.
AI processing & clip creation
Once ingested, the system separates audio and video streams. The audio is analysed and segmented into name 
announcements, identifying when each graduate is presented. In parallel, the video is analysed to detect key visual 
markers (such as when a graduate steps onto the stage, crosses it, and exits) across multiple camera angles.
These elements are then intelligently recombined, aligning the correct audio announcement with the corresponding 
video sequence to generate a personalised clip. This replaces what was traditionally a manual editing process, 
enabling accurate clip creation at scale and in near real time.
Validation & delivery
To ensure accuracy, an AI-assisted validation layer is built into the user journey. Graduates are presented with one or 
more thumbnail options based on system conﬁdence and conﬁrm their identity. Once validated, the personalised clip is 
instantly delivered via a mobile-ﬁrst platform, optimised for social sharing.
Because this process is enabled by First Sight Media’s rapid transfer of footage, clips are often delivered while 
graduates are still in the ceremony hall, maximising engagement and sharing.
Post-event engagement & insight
Alongside delivery, the platform introduces a short, targeted survey, capturing valuable insight at the point of highest 
engagement. This data is analysed within the platform to generate actionable insights, helping universities better 
understand and grow their graduate communities.
In practice, this collaboration creates a seamless end-to-end system where production and AI operate as a single 
workﬂow, transforming complex live event data into meaningful, shareable moments in real time.

Questions / Answers
What type of AI model or technique does your solution use? Please provide a simple, high-level explanation that
helps judges understand the approach (no conﬁdential or proprietary details required).
MyGraduationClip uses a hybrid AI approach, combining multiple techniques to analyse and transform live event 
footage into personalised video content at scale.
1. Speech Recognition and Audio Analysis
The system uses automatic speech recognition (ASR) to analyse ceremony audio and identify likely name-
announcement segments. Because live audio can be noisy and names are often pronounced in diﬀerent ways, we do 
not rely on a ﬁxed name list. Instead, we use similarity-based matching techniques (such as trigram and TF-IDF 
comparisons) to align registered graduate names with detected speech segments.
2. Computer Vision and Motion Detection
In parallel, computer vision techniques are used to analyse the video feed. The system detects patterns such as 
individuals entering and leaving the frame, movement across the stage, and changes in camera angle. These signals 
allow the platform to identify candidate “graduate moment” sequences without relying on predeﬁned zones or rigid 
ﬁlming setups.
3. Temporal Pattern Recognition
Graduation ceremonies follow highly consistent structures. The system uses temporal clustering and pattern analysis 
to group related video segments, even across multiple camera angles. This enables reconstruction of a coherent 
sequence for each graduate, regardless of how the footage is captured.
4. Conﬁdence Scoring and Matching
The system applies conﬁdence scoring to align audio and video segments and determine the most likely match for 
each graduate. Higher-conﬁdence matches are processed automatically, while lower-conﬁdence cases are ﬂagged for 
validation.
5. Human-in-the-Loop Validation
Where ambiguity exists, users are presented with a small number of ranked thumbnail options to conﬁrm their identity. 
This ensures accuracy without compromising scalability, combining automation with user validation.
6. Supporting AI for Analytics and Moderation
While the core clip-generation pipeline is based on multimodal analysis, additional AI techniques (including large 
language models) are used within the platform to support survey moderation and analytics, helping institutions 
interpret engagement data.
Within the collaboration, First Sight Media’s production workﬂow plays a critical supporting role by ensuring high-
quality, well-structured input data is delivered rapidly into the system. This enables the AI to operate eﬀectively in real-
world conditions and at the speed required to deliver real-time results.
Overall, the solution is best described as a hybrid, multimodal AI system combining speech recognition, computer 
vision, similarity matching, temporal clustering, and conﬁdence-based validation - designed to transform complex live-
event data into accurate, scalable, and instantly shareable content.
