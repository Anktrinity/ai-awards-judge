Description of your technology
Every event loses knowledge the moment the session ends. Voxo is the platform built to make sure that never happens 
again.
At its core, Voxo captures live audio from every stage simultaneously, processing each session through a proprietary 
pipeline that combines transcription, AI content generation, quality assurance, and branded publishing, all within 
minutes of a session ending. What would take an in-house production team months to deliver, Voxo produces before 
the next speaker has even left the stage.
The Intelligence Layer
Voxo is LLM-agnostic by design, integrating with OpenAI, Google Gemini, Anthropic Claude, or client-mandated 
models such as Salesforce Einstein. The platform ingests raw audio, generates structured transcripts, and applies 
multiple layers of natural language processing to extract key takeaways, speaker quotes, thematic clusters, and 
editorial-quality summaries in a single automated ﬂow.
The Product Suite
Every output is immediately branded in the event's visual identity and ready to publish. AI Session Summaries are 
delivered within minutes of each session concluding, available in desktop, mobile, and LinkedIn-optimised formats. AI 
Podcasts transform the same content into ﬁve-minute conversational audio recaps, with sponsor announcements 
woven seamlessly into the conversation. Daily and full Event Reports consolidate intelligence from every stage into 
executive-ready documents, complete with buzzword counters and deep-dive articles on trending topics. For multi-
track events, Theme Reports give each content stream its own dedicated, sponsor-ready publication.
On-site, Voxo extends beyond the screen. Digital signage surfaces real-time highlights between talks, while Live AI 
Commenting summarises sessions minute by minute and streams those insights to venue screens or attendees' 
mobile devices, ensuring no one misses a key moment regardless of which room they are in. Each speaker also walks 
away with a polished AI summary of their own session, ready to share with their networks in one click.
Human-in-the-Loop Quality Assurance
What separates Voxo from raw AI output is its dedicated QA layer. Every piece of content is reviewed by a human team 
before publishing: company and product names veriﬁed, speaker titles conﬁrmed, and speaker quotes audio-checked 
against original transcripts. The result is content that carries the editorial integrity of a professional publications team, 
produced at AI speed.
Integration, Reach, and ROI
Voxo is built to live inside existing event ecosystems rather than sit alongside them. The platform embeds directly into 
event apps via iFrame or unique session URLs, with native integrations spanning RainFocus, Swapcard, Cvent, Grip, 
ExpoPlatform, Swoogo, Amego, and more. Audio streaming piggybacks on existing AV infrastructure, making technical 
setup seamless.
All content lives in a fully branded Content Hub where every download is tracked and every share is measured. 
Organisers gain granular insight into which sessions resonated most, shaping future programming with real data. Voxo 
also opens entirely new commercial models through sponsor-branded summaries and micro-ticketing, transforming the 
post-event archive into an active revenue stream.
The result is an end-to-end AI content infrastructure that makes events immortal, sponsors measurable, and 
knowledge unstoppable.

Questions / Answers
Explain how the AI works in your solution?
Voxo's AI content platform operates through a collaborative partnership model where the AI supplier works closely with 
the event organiser throughout the entire event lifecycle. The technology itself is a real-time content generation system 
that processes live audio and produces multiple output products—summaries, podcasts, reports, and branded assets. 
What distinguishes the collaboration model is that the AI supplier is not a black-box vendor, but an active partner at 
every stage. Pre-event, Voxo's team works with the organiser on project planning, brand design, AV coordination, and 
marketing strategy. During the event, Voxo's team is there to monitor production, ensure quality, and adapt to changing 
conditions. Post-event, Voxo provides analytics and strategic guidance to help organisers understand what worked 
and plan for future events. The AI system itself piggybags on existing AV infrastructure rather than requiring 
deployment of new equipment. This reduces technical complexity and cost. The platform integrates natively with the 
organiser's event tech stackand the integration is seamless. Attendee data, session information, speaker details all 
ﬂow automatically from the event system into the AI pipeline. The result is a partnership where the AI supplier functions 
almost as an extension of the organiser's team.
What speciﬁc problem does this AI solve?
Event organisers and the agencies that manage events have traditionally faced a diﬃcult choice when considering AI-
content services. Option one: deploy an AI vendor at the event, but treat them as a vendor. The vendor delivers their 
software, provides limited support, and the organiser is responsible for success. If something goes wrong, if the output 
quality is poor, if the integration with the event tech stack fails, the organiser is left to troubleshoot alone. Option two: 
don't use AI services at all, and accept that event content will be ephemeral. The traditional vendor-buyer relationship 
creates distance and misaligned incentives. The vendor's goal is to deploy the product. The organiser's goal is to 
create exceptional events with lasting impact. Voxo solves this problem by adopting a partnership model rather than a 
vendor-buyer model. The AI supplier becomes an extension of the organiser's team, sharing responsibility for success. 
Voxo's team works with the organiser from initial planning through post-event analysis. They understand the 
organiser's goals, constraints, and priorities. When something goes wrong, they troubleshoot together. When an 
opportunity emerges to create exceptional content, they collaborate to execute it. This partnership approach aligns 
incentives and ensures everyone is working toward the same goals.
What makes your approach diﬀerent or innovative?
Voxo's collaborative partnership model is innovative in how it transforms the relationship between technology providers 
and event organisers. Historically, technology vendors have been arms-length relationships. You buy the software, the 
vendor supports it, and that's the extent of the partnership. Voxo inverts this model by making the relationship 
collaborative and ongoing. The partnership begins pre-event with project planning meetings where Voxo's team learns 
about the organiser's goals, audiences, speakers, and success metrics. Design and Marketing sessions ensure that 
generated content reﬂects the organiser's visual identity and messaging. This pre-event investment ensures that when 
the event runs, everything is optimized. During the event, Voxo's team monitors production in real-time. If a session is 
experiencing technical diﬃculties, they troubleshoot. If content quality is lower than expected, they investigate and 
improve. If new opportunities emerge—a session is resonating particularly strongly with attendees—they suggest ways 
to amplify that content. Post-event, Voxo provides detailed analytics showing which content performed best, which 
sessions resonated, which topics drove engagement. This data becomes the foundation for the organiser's strategy for 
future events. The innovation is that the AI supplier functions as a strategic partner, not just a technology vendor.

How did you test, validate, or quality-check the AI outputs to ensure they were accurate, safe, or reliable?
Voxo's quality assurance process is comprehensive, combining automated screening with expert human review. Every 
generated summary undergoes validation against the original session recording for accuracy. Speaker names and titles 
are veriﬁed using oﬃcial event directories and LinkedIn. Company and product names are checked for correct spelling 
and context. All direct quotations are audio-veriﬁed to ensure they are exact, in proper context, and accurately 
represent the speaker's position. Imagery and visual assets are reviewed for quality, accuracy, and brand compliance. 
The QA process scales across concurrent sessions through a tiered approach where automated systems catch 
obvious errors and human reviewers verify accuracy and quality standards.

Post-event execution continues the partnership. Voxo provides detailed analytics and insights, enabling organisers to

What distinguishes this execution model is its consistency. Regardless of event size, format, or complexity, the same

MODEL TRANSPARENCY (optional)
What type of AI model or technique does your solution use? Please provide a simple, high-level explanation that
helps judges understand the approach (no conﬁdential or proprietary details required).
Voxo's platform is built on model-agnostic architecture supporting multiple large language model providers. Customers 
choose OpenAI, Google Gemini, Anthropic Claude, or Salesforce Einstein based on preferences, cost, and 
requirements. This ﬂexibility ensures customers control infrastructure and avoid vendor lock-in. The technical pipeline 
begins with audio capture from existing AV infrastructure. Automatic speech recognition converts session audio into 
detailed text transcripts with speaker identiﬁcation. These transcripts feed into the chosen large language model with 
customized prompts designed for each content type. For summaries, prompts extract key points and preserve 
quotations. For podcasts, prompts generate conversational scripts. For reports, prompts aggregate sessions and 
identify insights. All prompts avoid hallucination and maintain accuracy with safeguards against bias. Generated 
content undergoes human-in-the-loop QA with automated systems screening errors and human reviewers verifying 
accuracy. The complete audit trail of all generation and QA decisions maintains compliance and transparency, allowing 
customers to see exactly how the AI system is performing and what quality measures are in place.
