Description of your technology
Rozie Synopsis is an AI‑native event platform that does two big jobs on one system: 1) Captures, ampliﬁes, and 
monetizes session knowledge, and 2) Activates exhibitor leads from the show ﬂoor. Instead of content and booth 
conversations disappearing after the event, it turns them into multilingual insights, knowledge hubs, structured leads, 
and clear ROI dashboards.
On stages, Rozie Synopsis plugs into the AV system and listens to live sessions. Real‑time speech recognition (around 
95–97% accuracy in professional AV environments) turns audio into text, and large language models generate live 
insights, concise summaries, key takeaways, recommendations, topic clusters, visual insights, and daily trends from 
those transcripts. In parallel, neural machine translation supports up to 75+ languages, so attendees can see captions 
and short recaps in their own language on venue screens, in the event app, or on their phones. This stack eﬀectively 
becomes a language and insight layer over the content program, making complex sessions easier to follow and reuse.
After the event, all sessions ﬂow into a branded, access‑controlled knowledge hub. This hub includes executive 
summaries, track debriefs, individual session reports, and audio highlights, so content teams start from AI‑generated 
assets instead of a blank page. An AI knowledge advisor sits on top of this content; attendees and teams can ask 
natural‑language questions such as “What were the main AI regulation themes?” and receive answers using only 
veriﬁed transcripts and reports, powered by an agentic AI framework. Organizers can also see which topics, sessions, 
and sponsors drive engagement and use that data to shape programming and sales strategies. Organizers also get 
access to the AI knowledge studio, which is pre-populated with all event data that they can use to re-purpose event 
knowlegde into blogs, infographics, and newsletters within minutes. 
On the show ﬂoor, Rozie Synopsis becomes an AI workspace for exhibitors. Booth staﬀ capture leads via badge or QR 
scans, business cards, short notes, or voice memos, and can optionally capture full conversations in voice format with 
consent. The AI then recaps each interaction, tags buyer intent, products discussed, objections, and next steps, and 
ﬁlls a structured record based on custom or pre‑built templates. These records sync in real time into exhibitor CRMs 
and can trigger personalized follow‑up emails and tasks, dramatically improving speed‑to‑lead and giving sales teams 
high‑quality context for every contact.
All of this runs on one uniﬁed AI platform designed for live event conditions. Operator‑ﬁrst UX, self‑healing behavior 
when connectivity drops, integrations with event apps, agenda tools, CRMs, and exhibitor portals, plus built‑in 
sponsorship inventory around insights, hubs, and analytics make Rozie Synopsis a practical way for organizers to turn 
events into ongoing, AI‑powered knowledge and revenue engines rather than one‑time experiences.

How did you test, validate, or quality-check the AI outputs to ensure they were accurate, safe, or reliable?
We treat accuracy, safety, and reliability as core product requirements because Rozie Synopsis runs in live, high‑stakes 
event environments.
1. Benchmarking the AI stack
We benchmark transcription and translation against leading enterprise systems using real conference‑style audio. In 
professional AV environments, we consistently see 95–97% transcription accuracy and 80–90% translation accuracy 
across 75+ languages, with sub‑10‑second latency from speech to on‑screen insight and 99.9% uptime across 50+ 
deployments.
2. Event‑speciﬁc conﬁguration and dry runs
Before each event, we conﬁgure domain guardrails like glossaries of acronyms, industry terms, speaker names, and 
brand language, plus standardized templates for recaps and reports. We do a dry‑run with AV teams to validate audio 
quality, latency, and end‑to‑end signal ﬂow, and adjust inputs (mics, mixer levels, ﬁlters) to minimize noise and 
crosstalk.
3. Guardrails, grounding, and ﬁlters
Generative outputs are grounded only in veriﬁed sources (session transcripts and structured metadata) using 
event‑speciﬁc prompts and agentic infrastructure for the Knowledge Advisor, which reduces hallucinations and keeps 
answers traceable.
Post‑processing ﬁlters check for oﬀensive, oﬀ‑topic, or unsafe content and trigger re‑runs if needed, while 
foundation‑model safety layers operate underneath and we avoid using personal demographic data.
4. Human‑in‑the‑loop editorial review
Organizers can choose random‑sample or full editorial review of key outputs such as executive summaries, daily 
debriefs, and ﬂagship session reports. Our editorial teams validate factual accuracy, tone, structure, and brand 
alignment, editing or regenerating sections quickly where needed.
5. Operational monitoring and self‑healing
Real‑time dashboards track session status, queue health, processing times, translation jobs, engagement, and 
exhibitor lead ﬂows, with alerts for anomalies.
If connectivity drops, Rozie Synopsis continues capturing raw audio and resumes processing when the network 
returns; self‑healing behavior addresses most transient failures without human intervention. 
AV and exhibitor teams consistently report that Rozie Synopsis is among the easiest tools to run on show days, which 
is reinforced by our ability to run hundreds of exhibitor workspaces and full content programs with no critical failures.
6. Enterprise‑grade practices and external validation
We operate with SOC‑II‑ready security practices, conﬁgurable US/EU data residency, clear data‑ownership boundaries, 
and opt‑in consent for recorded booth conversations.
Our approach has been validated by enterprises like Google, who selected Rozie Synopsis at their ﬂagship event.

Execution
Execution focuses on making powerful AI practical for AV teams, organizers, and exhibitors, not just impressive in 
demos. We design every step, pre‑event, during event, and post‑event, around real event operations so the system 
runs reliably at show speed.
Pre‑event setup
Before each event, we ingest agendas, session metadata, and sponsor information via templates or integrations with 
agenda tools, event apps, and registration systems. We conﬁgure domain guardrails (glossaries of acronyms, industry 
terms, speaker names, brand language) and agree on report templates, Knowledge Hub access rules, and sponsor 
placements. For the exhibitor experience, organizers invite exhibitors, deﬁne capture templates and qualiﬁcation ﬁelds, 
and support simple CRM connections so records can sync automatically.
Execution on stages
On site, AV teams route a clean mixed audio feed into Rozie Synopsis capture devices or laptops positioned at the AV 
desk. The live pipeline handles transcription, translation, and insight generation with 95–97% transcription accuracy, 
80–90% translation accuracy across 75+ languages, and sub‑10‑second latency from speech to on‑screen insight. 
Real‑time insights (key quotes, topics) and multilingual recaps are pushed to venue screens, in‑app embeds, and 
mobile web links, letting attendees follow along even when rooms are full or sessions overlap. After each session, the 
platform auto‑generates structured outputs like recaps, takeaways, recommendations, topic clusters, and end‑of‑day 
debriefs, that can be lightly spot‑checked. After the event Knowledge Hub with reports, audio summaries and AI 
knowledge advisor is auto-generated.
Execution on the show ﬂoor
For Exhibitor Lead Activation, exhibitors log into an AI workspace from phones, tablets, or laptops. After/before each 
booth conversation, they scan a badge/QR/business card, jot notes, or record a short voice memo (with optional full 
conversation recording on consent); AI converts this into a structured record with summaries, buyer intent, objections, 
and next steps. These records are created against event‑speciﬁc templates and sync into exhibitor CRMs in near real 
time, with optional triggers for personalized follow‑up emails and sales tasks.
Monitoring, reliability, and support
During the event, Rozie Synopsis provides live dashboards covering session status, processing queues, latency, 
engagement with insights, Knowledge Hub usage, booth conversations, lead volumes, and top topics. Self‑healing 
behavior continues capturing raw audio when connectivity drops and reprocesses content when the network is 
restored, while health checks and alerts notify on‑site or remote support if anomalies appear. AV and exhibitor teams 
consistently report that the system is among the easiest to run on show days, thanks to operator‑ﬁrst UX, clear 
playbooks, and brief dry runs before doors open.
Post‑event execution and scaling
Post‑event, all session outputs are organized into the branded Knowledge Hub with executive summaries, track 
debriefs, session reports, audio highlights, and the AI Knowledge Advisor. Organizers receive sponsor‑ready analytics 
decks covering content engagement, attendee behavior, exhibitor performance, and lead ﬂows, while exhibitors work 
their AI‑structured pipelines in CRM. This execution model has been repeated and reﬁned across 50+ events and 
hundreds of exhibitor workspaces, including complex multi‑day shows like DTECH and multi‑year agreements such as 
Clarion Events North America, demonstrating that the AI is operationally robust.
