Questions / Answers
Description of your technology
“Seek” turns your event ﬂoor into a game. Participants earn points by ﬁnding and photographing items from a curated 
list, and AI validates every submission in real time. Live leaderboards drive competition from ﬁrst session to closing 
keynote. Custom game logic, optional fortune-teller bonus features, and Gemini-powered scoring making every game 
unique to the event. Each attendee has a unique experience because the images they are capturing are all diﬀerent, 
chosen by the attendee to meet the challenge. They are able to choose the order in which they complete the tasks, 
choose the best image and take the photo they feel the AI will judge in a positive manner and in some cases get a 
diﬀerent level of points depending on the image they choose to upload.
TECHNOLOGY DESCRIPTION
Explain how the AI works in your solution?
A scavenger hunt where AI does the judging, dynamic scoring, live leaderboards, and
Gemini-powered validation that makes the whole event ﬂoor a playing ﬁeld.
• Attendees accessed "Seek" through their event app (also provided by Gramercy Tech)
• Attendee is given a list of tasks to complete (for example, ﬁnd a "sweet treat")
• Powered by Gemini, AI validates photo submissions dynamically
• Parameters are set on the back end to help the AI judge (i.e. don't accept photos of a
screen, etc)
• If AI does not think you completed the task, Gemini will give you feedback on why (You
did not include the correct image type, etc.)
• Once tasks are completed, attendees receive points and climbs up the leaderboard
What speciﬁc problem does this AI solve?
UPS needed a more dynamic way to engage attendees beyond passive programming.
Traditional activations often struggle to sustain attention, encourage exploration, or spark
meaningful interaction across large-scale events.
This AI-powered experience transformed the event into an active, game-driven environment.
By dynamically updating tasks each day, it kept engagement fresh while guiding attendees
to discover new areas of the space; from scanning a dinner napkin at the gala to connecting
with members of the leadership team, to even ﬁnding shades of UPS brown throughout the
event.
AI validation removed the need for manual oversight, enabling real-time participation at
scale while ensuring fairness and consistency. In doing so, the solution didn’t just increase
engagement, it fostered organic networking, movement throughout the venue, and a sense
of friendly competition that extended across the entire event.
It also allowed each individual to have an unique experience. Problem solving, working alongside the AI and 
understanding there isn't just one "right answer" but the ability to be creative to work within the guidlines of the 
challenge created a personal journey for each and every attendee.

Questions / Answers
What makes your approach diﬀerent or innovative?
What sets this approach apart is the use of AI as a tool to actively guide attendee behavior in
real time. Rather than serving as a passive validator, AI became the tool through which
engagement was shaped; be it through directing movement, encouraging interaction, or
even reinforcing participation across the event.
Powered by Gemini, AI acted as a real-time judge, interpreting submissions, delivering
feedback, and dynamically assigning points. This transformed the experience from a static
scavenger hunt into a responsive system where each action prompted a clear, immediate
reaction, rewarding desired behaviors.
The experience was further diﬀerentiated by its progressive, multi-day structure, with AI
enabling new prompts to be deployed and validated seamlessly at scale. This allowed the
game to evolve alongside the event, sustaining engagement and keeping interactions fresh.
By positioning AI as both guide and judge, the solution redeﬁned its role within live
experiences, not just as a backend tool, but as a central driver of behavior, engagement, and
storytelling.
VALIDATION
How did you test, validate, or quality-check the AI outputs to ensure they were accurate, safe, or reliable?
We implemented a multi-layered validation approach to ensure accuracy, safety, and
reliability. At the core, Gemini was guided by structured positive and negative prompts to
deﬁne what constitutes a valid submission while explicitly ﬁltering out disallowed or
irrelevant content (i.e. screens, NSFW imagery).
On the backend, we established task-speciﬁc parameters and edge-case scenarios to test
how the AI responded across a range of inputs. Our team conducted iterative QA testing
prior to launch, submitting both valid and intentionally incorrect images to evaluate
consistency, reﬁne prompts, and reduce false positives/negatives.
We also designed the system to provide clear, real-time feedback to users when submissions
were rejected, improving transparency and guiding correct behavior. Together, these
measures ensured the AI operated reliably at scale while maintaining a safe and controlled
experience for all attendees.
IMPACT
In one summary, explain the overall impact of your AI solution. Please cover four areas: innovation, execution, user or
customer outcomes, and measurable results.
Innovation
Rather than using AI as a passive validation tool, this solution positioned it as an active
participant in the experience. Powered by Gemini, AI served as the real-time judge:
interpreting submissions, delivering feedback, and dynamically assigning points.
This transformed the interaction from a simple checklist into a responsive system that
guided behavior, rewarded creativity, and made the AI itself part of the gameplay. By giving
attendees visibility into how decisions were made, the experience demystiﬁed AI while
inviting them into the process in a playful, accessible way.

Execution
The experience was designed in close collaboration with UPS, beginning with a shared
deﬁnition of success around two key objectives: sustaining engagement across multiple days
and increasing meaningful attendee interaction.
Through this process, we aligned on speciﬁc behaviors to encourage ongoing participation,
movement throughout the event, and organic networking. To support this, the game was
structured as a progressive journey, with new tasks released daily rather than all at once.
This created a sense of momentum and encouraged attendees to return and re-engage
throughout the event.
Tasks were intentionally mapped to key moments and locations across the program, from
gala elements to leadership interactions, and included prompts designed to foster
connection, such as identifying and engaging with speciﬁc attendees or leadership ﬁgures.
In doing so, the entire event environment became part of the experience.
On the backend, AI parameters and prompt structures were carefully conﬁgured to maintain
accuracy, consistency, and safety at scale, enabling seamless real-time participation without
manual oversight.
Outcome
The result was a shift from passive attendance to active, meaningful participation. Attendees
were encouraged to move through the space, discover new areas, and engage with one
another in ways that felt organic rather than forced.
By embedding networking into gameplay, such as tasks requiring interaction with leadership
or peers, the experience lowered social barriers and created natural moments of connection.
At the same time, many of the prompts were intentionally designed to reﬂect elements of
UPS’s culture, values, and internal ecosystem.
This transformed the experience from simple engagement into something more resonant:
attendees weren’t just completing tasks, they were interacting with the people, symbols, and
touchpoints that deﬁne the company. The result was a stronger sense of connection; not only
to each other, but to the broader UPS ethos: fostering pride, recognition, and a shared sense
of belonging across the event.
Results
The activation achieved meaningful internal visibility, with UPS CEO Carol Tomé
participating in the experience, signaling strong alignment and engagement at the highest
level of the organization.
It also delivered the highest level of engagement to date, with approximately 35% of
attendees actively participating. This marked a 25% increase year over year within a
company culture where gamiﬁed activations are still emerging. This level of adoption
reﬂects both the accessibility of the experience and its ability to resonate across a broad
attendee base.
Qualitative feedback further reinforced its impact, with both attendees and the event team
noting that the experience felt more engaging and socially connective than previous
iterations. Many participants highlighted the way it encouraged interaction, discovery, and a
sense of shared experience that felt aligned with UPS’s people-ﬁrst culture.

Questions / Answers
What type of AI model or technique does your solution use? Please provide a simple, high-level explanation that
helps judges understand the approach (no conﬁdential or proprietary details required).
We bridged the gap between physical participation and digital rewards by integrating Google Gemini into our event 
ecosystem. Using a library of proprietary prompt frameworks, we transformed the AI from a simple chatbot into a 
sophisticated visual judge capable of analyzing attendee photos with human-level nuance and machine-level speed.
Key Technical Capabilities
Dynamic Visual Intelligence: Our proprietary prompts allow the AI to perform granular, multi-scale analysis. It can 
identify speciﬁc objects, brand colors, and even ""Networking Bingo"" milestones. By utilizing high-velocity OCR 
(Optical Character Recognition), Gemini identiﬁes names and geographic data printed on attendee badges, instantly 
verifying connections between participants from diﬀerent states or industries.
AI ""Intuition"" & Spatial Reasoning: We pushed the boundaries of standard AI identiﬁcation by utilizing Gemini’s ability 
to make logical assumptions. During the event attendees submitted photos of packages. Our AI didn't just tag the 
package; it estimated the package volume and weight. Points were then awarded dynamically based on these 
estimates - for example, a large box earned 100 points creating a gamiﬁed experience that felt personalized and 
responsive.
Hyper-Localized Inclusion: To ensure a truly global experience, we leveraged Gemini’s native ﬂuency in dozens of 
languages. This allowed us to provide real-time, context-aware feedback and greetings to every attendee in their native 
tongue. Whether a participant was from Tokyo, Paris, or São Paulo, the AI acted as a multilingual concierge, ensuring 
the ""magic"" of the event was accessible to all.
The Impact
By removing the ""human-in-the-loop"" requirement for veriﬁcation, we achieved:
Zero Latency: Attendees received instant gratiﬁcation and leaderboard updates, sustaining high engagement levels 
throughout the event.
Operational Eﬃciency: We eliminated the need for dedicated ""scoring stations,"" allowing staﬀ to focus on high-touch 
hospitality rather than administrative veriﬁcation.
Global Scalability: The AI’s linguistic versatility allowed us to support a diverse, international audience without the 
overhead of a human translation team.
