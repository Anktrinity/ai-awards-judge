Description of your technology
Digital Doppelgänger turn event planning from assumption-based decision-making into simulation-driven stratey. It is an AI-powered 
decision intelligence solution that enables organizations to simulate audience reactions before any communication or events goes live. 
It allows you to test how audiences will react, before committing budget, time and production resources.
The system creates psychologically grounded behavioral models of real audiences. Instead of delivering a single answer, it generates 
decision-tested scenarios that make trade-oﬀs, risks and likely outcomes transparent. This replaces a core weakness in the industry: 
Rather than relying only on assumptions, post-event feedback or costly upfront research, Digital Doppelgänger close this gap and allow 
organizations to test concepts, messages, formats and strategic choices in advance. The system shows how diﬀerent audience types 
respond, where engagement is created, where friction may occur and which elements are most likely to drive action, trust or 
disengagement.
At its core, the solution combines behavioral science, structured audience modeling and AI-powered simulation. Each Digital 
Doppelgänger reﬂects stable human drivers such as values, motivations, decision patterns and context-speciﬁc expectations. These 
models are applied to speciﬁc use cases, for example live events, trade shows, internal communication formats or brand experiences. 
You can compare alternatives, challenge assumptions and improve decision quality before production begins. Instead of waiting for 
feedback after execution, you can evaluate strategic options based on likely behavioral responses during the planning phase.
The product was built to solve a common problem in communication and event design: critical decisions are often made before 
organizations truly know how their audiences will react. Existing methods either provide feedback too late or require expensive and 
time-consuming research. Digital Doppelgänger close this gap by adding a practical simulation layer between strategy and execution. 
The result is a fundamental shift from assumption-based planning to simulation-driven decision-making, reducing uncertainty, 
accelerating alignment and improving decision quality.  Instead of asking “Will this work?”, you can now test it. 
Unlike persona-based approached or prompt-driven simulations, Digital Doppelgänger are built on structured weighted bahavioral 
models that deﬁne decision logic independently from the AI system generating the output. Digital Doppelgänger do not replace creative 
thinking. They strengthen it you allowing ideas to be tested, reﬁned and validated before exection. 
TECHNOLOGY DESCRIPTION
Explain how the AI works in your solution?
Digital Doppelgänger combine large language models with a structured behavioral framework to simulate how a speciﬁc audience 
thinks and decides. The AI operates within deﬁned behavioral boundaries.
At the core is a behavioral structured model based on stable human drivers such as values, motivations, risk tolerance, information 
preferences and decision patterns. Public signals, domain knowledge and structured inputs are translated into measurable behavioral 
dimensions. These dimensions deﬁne how a target group processes information and reacts to strategic choices in a speciﬁc scenario. 
Each model uses weighted variables. This means behavioral traits are calibrated. The same audience model can therefore react 
diﬀerently depending on the scenario, while still staying consistent in its underlying logic.
When users test a concept, the language model generates responses within these predeﬁned parameters. It does not “invent” a 
personality. It executes the decision logic deﬁned by the behavioral framework. This hybrid architecture combines generative AI with 
structured behavioral weighting. The result is consistent, context-aware simulation of likely audience reactions across communication 
and event scenarios.
The System is designed as a scalable and repeatable framework that can be applied across industries, event formats and international 
markets without requiring custom AI development for each use case.

How did you test, validate, or quality-check the AI outputs to ensure they were accurate, safe, or reliable?
Digital Doppelgänger were tested and validated through a structured, multi-layered process combining academic collaboration, expert 
review and real-world scenario testing. The goal was to ensure accuracy, stability and realistic audience simulation.
The behavioral modeling framework was developed in collaboration with academic partners and AI experts from the University of 
Wuppertal. Their expertise in marketing science and AI-driven synthetic market research supported the design, calibration and 
validation of the models. This ensured that core dimensions such as values, motivational drivers, decision logic and contextual 
sensitivity were grounded in established theory rather than assumptions. Validation took place on several levels.
First, structural validation. The weighted behavioral dimensions were continuously reﬁned to ensure internal consistency and logical 
coherence. We tested whether outputs remained stable when variables changed. This ensured that responses followed the deﬁned 
motivational logic and did not drift unpredictably.
Second, comparative scenario validation. The models were applied retrospectively to past communication and event projects. 
Simulated reactions were compared with documented real-world outcomes. Instead of feeding post-event data into the system, we 
replicated scenarios and checked whether the simulated behavior plausibly matched known results.
Third, expert review. Industry specialists and client-side stakeholders with deep audience knowledge reviewed the outputs. They 
evaluated whether the simulated reactions realistically reﬂected known audience characteristics. Their feedback was used to 
recalibrate weightings and contextual sensitivity.
Fourth, identity-based validation. In controlled settings, Digital Doppelgänger were built to reﬂect real individuals within an organization. 
These individuals reviewed the simulated responses and assessed whether they aligned with their own decision patterns and 
preferences. This created a direct qualitative benchmark for behavioral accuracy.
In addition, system-level safety checks were implemented. The structured behavioral layer constrains the generative AI component, 
reducing arbitrary outputs and ensuring responses stay within deﬁned parameters. Through academic grounding, iterative calibration, 
expert validation and controlled real-person testing, Digital Doppelgänger were systematically reﬁned to deliver consistent, plausible 
and decision-relevant simulations.

Questions / Answers
What type of AI model or technique does your solution use? Please provide a simple, high-level explanation that helps judges
understand the approach (no conﬁdential or proprietary details required).
Digital Doppelgänger combine structured behavioral modeling with large language models. In simple terms: a deﬁned audience logic 
guides a generative AI system to simulate how real target groups would decide in speciﬁc scenarios.
The system consists of two layers. First, a structured behavioral modeling layer. Second, a generative language model layer. The 
behavioral layer deﬁnes weighted psychological dimensions such as values, motivations, risk orientation, information preferences and 
decision patterns. These dimensions are organized into a stable conﬁguration that represents a speciﬁc target group. This structured 
proﬁle deﬁnes how the audience thinks and decides. It sets clear boundaries and response tendencies, ensuring that simulations 
remain consistent instead of varying randomly.
On top of this layer, a large language model (such as OpenAI, Google Gemini or Anthropic Claude) is used to generate scenario-based 
responses. The language model produces outputs within the deﬁned behavioral framework. It functions as an execution layer, while the 
behavioral model deﬁnes the decision logic and evaluation criteria. Each simulation follows a structured input–evaluation–response 
process. This allows systematic testing of diﬀerent scenarios rather than generating isolated outputs.
Because the behavioral modeling layer is independent from the underlying language model, the framework is system-agnostic. It can 
be deployed across diﬀerent AI providers without changing the conceptual structure. Current implementations primarily use leading 
OpenAI models for performance and stability, but the framework has been successfully adapted to other major AI systems.
No proprietary training of foundation models is required. The innovation lies in structured conﬁguration, contextual calibration and 
controlled simulation logic applied on top of existing generative AI infrastructure. This ensures portability, scalability and long-term 
adaptability as AI ecosystems evolve.
