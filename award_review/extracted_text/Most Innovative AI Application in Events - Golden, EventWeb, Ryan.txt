Description of your technology
ExhibitGen is EventWeb’s proprietary AI RFP Wizard, built to revolutionize the conceptualization and bidding phase of 
tradeshow exhibit design. Traditionally, event managers rely on text-heavy briefs and back-and-forth discovery calls, 
which often result in a costly "vision gap" between the client’s expectations and the producer’s initial designs. 
ExhibitGen solves this by integrating an AI-driven design generator directly into the RFP workﬂow.
Users are guided through a structured input process, deﬁning their needs, footprint, and aesthetic goals. In seconds, 
ExhibitGen translates these inputs into high-quality, conceptual visual renders. Crucially, the technology includes a 
"tweak and regenerate" feature, allowing account executives and clients to iterate on the ﬂy during discovery calls. 
Once a visual concept is locked in, ExhibitGen seamlessly integrates the output with EventWeb’s Producer Directory, 
instantly transitioning a visual concept into an actionable, multi-vendor request for quotation. It is not just an image 
generator; it is an end-to-end procurement acceleration tool.
Access and try out ExhibitGen at http://www.ExhibitGen.ai (http://www.ExhibitGen.ai) 
TECHNOLOGY DESCRIPTION
Explain how the AI works in your solution?
ExhibitGen utilizes a multimodal AI architecture combining natural language processing (NLP) with an advanced 
generative image model. The NLP layer parses the structured data from the user’s guided RFP inputs—such as booth 
dimensions, desired materials, interactive elements, and brand themes. It translates this data into highly optimized, 
weighted prompts. These prompts are then fed into our generative visual model, which has been ﬁne-tuned to 
understand spatial dynamics, lighting, and common architectural structures used in the events industry. The output is a 
high-ﬁdelity rendering that stakeholders can immediately review, modify via conversational text tweaks, and attach to 
their formal EventWeb bid requests.
What speciﬁc problem does this AI solve?
It solves the "vision gap" and the bottleneck of pre-sales design. Before a formal design team commits hours to CAD 
drawings and 3D models—often a sunk cost if the client goes another direction—stakeholders struggle to align on a 
shared visual language. ExhibitGen provides instant visual context. It allows account executives to close the 
conceptual gap on a single discovery call, securing client buy-in before heavy design and engineering resources are 
deployed.
What makes your approach diﬀerent or innovative?
While other AI image generators exist in a vacuum, ExhibitGen is speciﬁcally contextualized for the business of events. 
Our innovation lies in the workﬂow integration. The AI doesn't just create a compelling image; the generated render is 
tethered to EventWeb’s procurement mechanics. We recognize that AI is a tool for aesthetic alignment, not ﬁnal 
engineering. Once the visual direction is locked in, users easily transition from ideation to action. They utilize their 
generated concepts to source a compatible producer from our directory and attach the visuals directly to their formal 
RFP. It turns AI from a simple brainstorming novelty into a highly functional bridge between conceptualization and 
actionable vendor sourcing, signiﬁcantly reducing friction without forcing users into a rigid pipeline.

Questions / Answers
How did you test, validate, or quality-check the AI outputs to ensure they were accurate, safe, or reliable?
Our validation process focused on practical usability and aesthetic alignment rather than ﬁnal engineering readiness. 
Because ExhibitGen is designed as an ideation tool—not a replacement for structural CAD design—our testing 
prioritized the AI's ability to interpret event-speciﬁc prompts and generate relevant visual concepts.
We conducted extensive internal "prompt-to-reality" testing, running over 50 rigorous, varied RFP scenarios to 
evaluate the outputs. We monitored how the model handled complex requests like multi-level booths, speciﬁc lighting 
moods, and brand color integration. During this testing phase, we identiﬁed that while the AI excels at rapid 
visualization, it occasionally generates structurally ambitious or "wild" concepts. We view this as a feature of the 
brainstorming process, not a bug. To account for this, the platform is explicitly framed to ensure users understand 
these renders are aesthetic starting points that require human guidance and professional reﬁnement. By setting clear 
expectations and rigorously testing the prompt boundaries, we validated that the tool successfully serves its primary 
purpose: eliminating the "blank page" problem and accelerating visual alignment before formal engineering begins. Our 
terms of service explicitly separate this conceptual generation from the liability of ﬁnal, structurally sound designs.
IMPACT
In one summary, explain the overall impact of your AI solution. Please cover four areas: innovation, execution, user or
customer outcomes, and measurable results.
Innovation
ExhibitGen represents a paradigm shift from asynchronous, manual design brieﬁngs to synchronous, AI-assisted visual 
alignment. It democratizes the initial design phase, allowing non-technical account managers to visually express 
complex ideas instantly.
Execution
The execution strategy for ExhibitGen was centered on intuitive integration rather than forced adoption. Event 
professionals already manage complex logistics, so introducing a completely separate, standalone tool would create 
unnecessary friction. Instead, we embedded the AI generation directly into the existing EventWeb user journey.
As users navigate the standard RFP creation process, they are naturally intercepted and oﬀered the option to utilize the 
AI Wizard. This contextual placement ensures the technology feels like an extension of their workﬂow rather than an 
interruption. We designed the interface to be highly accessible, requiring zero prior prompt-engineering experience. 
The guided input forms translate everyday event terminology into optimized AI prompts behind the scenes. This 
frictionless execution strategy allows users to instantly see the value of translating their text-based requirements into 
high-ﬁdelity 3D conceptual renderings without leaving the procurement environment.

Outcome

the event planning ecosystem: the buyers and the builders.

procurement phase, ensuring clients feel conﬁdent that their visual expectations are clearly communicated.
Equally important are the outcomes for account managers and sales teams at event production companies. For these 
professionals, ExhibitGen serves as a powerful onboarding and pre-sales tool. When an account manager receives a 
new lead, they can use the Wizard during live discovery calls to generate concepts in real-time. This interactive 
process entirely transforms the initial client meeting from a standard Q&A session into a collaborative design 
workshop. By bridging the "vision gap" immediately, account managers can secure client buy-in much faster. It fosters 
a more collaborative, eﬃcient, and visually uniﬁed relationship between the buyer and the builder right from the ﬁrst 
interaction.
Results
As a newly launched addition to the EventWeb platform, our initial results are measured by the high quality and 
practical applicability of the visual outputs rather than long-term aggregated metrics. The most compelling evidence of 
the tool's immediate success is our extensive public gallery of generated examples.
These renders demonstrate the AI's remarkable capacity to interpret diverse industry requests—from sleek, minimalist 
technology pavilions to immersive activations. The gallery serves as a testament to the fact that the tool reliably 
produces high-ﬁdelity, presentation-ready concepts that rival traditional, time-consuming mood boards. By providing 
users with immediate access to these high-quality visuals, the platform is already proving its core value proposition: 
saving time and reducing miscommunication. The immediate, veriﬁable result for any user is the ability to attach a 
stunning, bespoke visual concept to their RFP, ensuring that the quotes they receive are based on a shared, concrete 
vision.

In this instance, the production account manager utilized ExhibitGen during the initial onboarding and discovery phase.

of high-quality conceptual renders in real-time. Instead of waiting days for a preliminary sketch, the client was able to 
react to the visuals instantly on the call, making conversational tweaks to the layout and branding elements until the 
digital representation matched their internal vision.
