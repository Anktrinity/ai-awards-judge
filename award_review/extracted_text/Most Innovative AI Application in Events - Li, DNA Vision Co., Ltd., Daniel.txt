Questions / Answers
Description of your technology
DNA Vision Pro is a closed-loop AI operating system for events. A venue can be captured through iPhone LiDAR, GLB 
input, or PDF/JPG ﬂoor plans. The system then runs AI layout across spatial conﬁrmation, stage positioning, audience 
area, FOH, lighting, power, and circulation. Assembly Intelligence converts dimensions into buildable structural BOM. 
DNA Constitution runs 93 rules before output. DNA Command brings together four ﬁnancial layers: actual closeout 
cost, supplier market pricing, prepayment control, and client selling price. After each event, real execution data ﬂows 
back into the system. The result is not just faster output, but a workﬂow where planning, costing, compliance, and 
post-event learning continuously reinforce one another.
TECHNOLOGY DESCRIPTION
Explain how the AI works in your solution?
DNA Vision Pro uses a hybrid AI architecture. A RAG layer retrieves the most relevant cases from 7,000 real event 
records to ground planning and pricing in real operational history. DNA Constitution applies 93 hard rules as Boolean 
constraints across global, regional, and national compliance logic. Deterministic decision logic handles structural, 
pricing, and safety-critical outputs. Generative AI is limited to language and document expression. Most importantly, 
the system is not one-directional: actual execution outcomes and closeout data ﬂow back into the platform, improving 
future recommendations and pricing accuracy.
What speciﬁc problem does this AI solve?
The core problem is not only that event planning is slow. It is that the workﬂow is fragmented. Venue planning, supplier 
costing, compliance judgment, quotation, and closeout learning usually sit in diﬀerent tools, diﬀerent people, and 
diﬀerent ﬁles. As a result, each event behaves like a fresh start, and the company rarely sees true commercial feedback 
in time. DNA Vision Pro solves this fragmentation by connecting planning, pricing, execution logic, and post-event truth 
inside one operating workﬂow.
What makes your approach diﬀerent or innovative?
DNA Vision Pro is innovative because it does not treat AI as a layer on top of event work. It redesigns the event 
company workﬂow itself. Venue input connects directly to AI layout. AI layout connects directly to structural BOM. 
Structural BOM connects directly to four-layer ﬁnancial logic. Real closeout data then feeds back into the system. This 
transforms event operations from a fragmented tool chain into a compounding operating system. It is not just faster 
software. It is workﬂow redesign.
VALIDATION
How did you test, validate, or quality-check the AI outputs to ensure they were accurate, safe, or reliable?
We validated the system across four dimensions. First, rule validation: all 93 DNA Constitution rules are based on real 
high-cost execution failures and checked against operational and regulatory logic. Second, quote back-testing: 500 
events were tested against real closeout outcomes, with pricing variance held within 15%. Third, structural validation: 
Assembly Intelligence outputs were checked against supplier speciﬁcations to conﬁrm buildability. Fourth, market 
validation: the product has already secured 40+ paid beta clients with 100% intent conversion among invited users. 
For this category, the most important proof is that the loop is real: planning outputs connect to execution, and 
execution results feed back into the system.

IMPACT
In one summary, explain the overall impact of your AI solution. Please cover four areas: innovation, execution, user or
customer outcomes, and measurable results.
Innovation
The innovation is not that DNA Vision Pro makes event proposals faster. It makes event companies smarter over time. 
The ﬁrst breakthrough is Four-Layer Cost Intelligence: proposal pricing, supplier market truth, prepayment control, and 
actual closeout cost are brought into one operational view. The second is the Post-Event Feedback Flywheel: each 
completed event improves the system’s future accuracy. The third is the integration of venue input, AI layout, BOM, 
compliance, costing, and closeout into a single operating sequence. This is why DNA Vision Pro should be seen not as 
a tool upgrade, but as a new operating model for event businesses.
Execution
Our execution approach focused ﬁrst on the most repeatable, highest-friction workﬂows: hotel ballrooms, brand galas, 
and corporate events. These scenarios allowed us to validate the full chain from venue input to layout, BOM, quotation, 
and closeout. We then designed the system so it could scale through a lightweight expansion model: headquarters 
maintains the core intelligence, while new cities only need local regulation, venue data, and market input to activate a 
new operating node. This makes the product structurally scalable rather than locally trapped.
Outcome
The outcome is a shift in how decisions are made. Event companies no longer need to rely only on memory, 
fragmented spreadsheets, or hindsight after the job is over. They can see commercial truth earlier, enforce risk logic 
before output, and use real closeout outcomes to improve future planning. In practical terms, this changes event 
management from reactive execution into predictive operating intelligence.
Results
DNA Vision Pro has already reduced standard planning workﬂows from 13 days to 30 seconds. Quote back-testing has 
held within 15% variance. The system has secured 40+ paid beta clients with 100% intent conversion among invited 
users. Supplier checks conﬁrmed BOM outputs were buildable. During paid beta usage, no ballroom layout violations 
requiring post-output revision were observed. More importantly, each completed event increases the intelligence of 
future outputs, proving that the closed loop is commercially active, not theoretical.
CASE STUDY
Describe how the AI was used in practice.
A representative use case is not just one event, but a full workﬂow transformation. In a hotel gala scenario, the venue 
plan is uploaded, the AI generates layout, identiﬁes operational conﬂicts, produces a structural BOM, and creates a 
four-layer quote. After the event is completed, actual execution cost and outcome data are written back into the 
system. This means each output is not the end of the process, but the start of a more accurate next one. That is the 
practical meaning of innovation in DNA Vision Pro.
