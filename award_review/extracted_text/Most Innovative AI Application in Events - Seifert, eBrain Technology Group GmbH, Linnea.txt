Description of your technology
eBrain is not an event tool with AI features. eBrain is an AI-centric operating model that redeﬁnes the entire operational 
logic of attendee management.
The event industry has digitized its processes - but it has not made them intelligent. Behind every successful event lies 
an invisible layer of fragmented systems, spreadsheet workarounds, manual change cascades, and time-consuming 
coordination. Event teams spend the majority of their working hours not on creative work and guest experience, but on 
administration. eBrain was built to solve this structural problem.
*Five-layer architecture:*
eBrain organizes event operations across ﬁve interconnected layers:
The Experience Layer encompasses event websites, registration, ticketing, forms, and check-in - everything 
participants and teams interact with directly. The Operations Layer manages attendees, contingents, hotel and transfer 
logic, communication, surveys, tasks, and change management. The Intelligence Layer embeds AI into every 
operational step: website automation, AI-assisted communication, reporting, NLP insights, forecasting, 
recommendations and Emily as the dialogic access and action layer - powered by an agentic architecture where the 
LLM reasons, selects tools, and chains actions autonomously. The Synchronization Layer serves as the single source 
of truth, keeping changes, statuses, and data consistent across all touchpoints - eliminating manual reconciliation 
cascades. The Trust & Compliance Layer ensures EU-sovereign hosting, GDPR compliance, and EU AI Act readiness.
*Four core engines across the full event lifecycle:*
The Setup Machine enables event creation, AI-powered website building via wizard and prompting, registration 
conﬁguration, and campaign launch. The Run Machine delivers a live dashboard with task management, real-time 
contingent management for hotels, sessions, shuttles, and catering, automated communication, and synchronized 
change management. The Onsite Machine handles badges, QR-based check-in, and live attendance status. The Learn 
Machine generates day-to-day reporting, KPI analytics, forecasting, NLP feedback insights, and historical benchmarks.
*Emily - the new interaction logic:*
Emily is not a chatbot feature. Emily is a dialogic access, guidance, and action layer. Event managers ask natural-
language questions ("How many vegetarians are registered?", "Which hotels are overbooked?"), receive immediate 
operational answers with action recommendations, and can trigger actions directly - such as sending a personalized 
VIP reminder in German and English. Emily makes even less experienced team members immediately eﬀective and 
dramatically reduces onboarding time.
The result is not a better tool. It is a new operating model: fewer tool switches, less manual coordination, fewer errors -  
and fundamentally more time for what event teams are actually here for: staging, guest quality, and strategic impact.

Explain how the AI works in your solution?
AI operates in eBrain across three clearly diﬀerentiated levels - not as a single feature, but as a continuous intelligence 
layer spanning the entire event operations value chain.
LLM level: High-performance language models generate event website content, optimize copy, translate across 
languages, process natural-language queries, and deliver contextual assistance. A corporate design crawler 
automatically extracts brand identity from existing web presences.
Assistant level: Specialized AI assistants guide users through every phase of attendee management — website 
building, communication creation, reporting, and operational questions. They deliver not just information, but 
contextual guidance and action recommendations adapted to the current event status and user role.
Agent level: Task-oriented AI agents support or trigger concrete operational steps — the CTA agent suggests daily 
prioritized to-dos, while other agents handle email creation, data reconciliation, and cross-touchpoint status updates.
Emily uniﬁes all three levels as the dialogic interface: users interact with the entire system through natural language, 
receiving answers, recommendations, and triggering actions — without context switches. The entire AI infrastructure 
runs on EU-sovereign cloud (IONOS), is GDPR-compliant by design, and addresses EU AI Act documentation and risk 
assessment requirements.
What speciﬁc problem does this AI solve?
The event industry does not have a creativity problem. It has an operational burden problem.
Existing attendee management tools have digitized legacy processes - but they have not made them intelligent. They 
replicate the same unintelligent workﬂows on a digital surface: fragmented data, manual change cascades, 
disconnected communication, spreadsheet reconciliation. The result is legacy thinking running on modern 
infrastructure — consuming capacity without generating value.
A single participant change - a cancellation, a room switch, a dietary update - triggers manual follow-up across ﬁve or 
more touchpoints: update the list, notify the hotel, adjust the shuttle plan, correct the badge, revise the catering count, 
adapt the communication. These cascades cost time, produce errors, and trap highly qualiﬁed teams in administrative 
labor instead of creative and strategic work.
Registration websites take days instead of minutes. Contingents and hotels are nearly impossible to steer in real time. 
Reporting happens after the event instead of during operations. Onboarding new team members takes too long 
because system logic is complex and never self-explanatory.
eBrain solves this structural problem by rebuilding the entire operational logic as AI-centric — synchronized, dialogic, 
action-oriented. The system relieves, guides, and orchestrates.

How did you test, validate, or quality-check the AI outputs to ensure they were accurate, safe, or reliable?
eBrain's validation strategy follows one principle: practical intelligence before lab perfection. Every AI application is 
measured against real operational impact, not technological ambition.
1. Foundation from 20+ years of operational validation
The underlying attendee management logic is not theoretical - it has been tested in real event projects across 
industries and complexity levels for over two decades. This operational experience deﬁnes where AI creates genuine 
leverage and where human control remains essential. eBrain was not built from technology enthusiasm, but from deep 
understanding of real event bottlenecks.
2. Surgical AI integration by use case
Rather than deploying AI generically, the most critical operational bottlenecks were analyzed individually: website 
building, list reconciliation, change management, communication, task orchestration, reporting, onsite processes. For 
each use case, the team deﬁned which AI intervention serves a clear business purpose and which quality parameters 
apply.
3. Three-level quality assurance
At the LLM level, generated content - website copy, communications, translations - is constrained by contextual 
parameters and brand logic. At the assistant level, recommendations and guidance are bound to actual event data - 
assistants work on real participant, contingent, and status data. At the agent level, triggered actions are presented as 
suggestions - ﬁnal decisions and approval remain with the human operator. This reﬂects eBrain’s commitment to 
responsible AI: technology that augments human judgment, maintains full operator control, and operates within clear 
ethical and regulatory boundaries.
4. Technical safety and security layer
The report generation engine includes a dedicated SQL veriﬁcation system: every AI-generated query is validated 
against table and column whitelists, blocked from executing DELETE, DROP, INSERT, or UPDATE operations, 
prevented from exposing internal record identiﬁers, and automatically scoped to the relevant event data - ensuring 
strict data isolation between clients. This veriﬁcation layer is covered by 16 automated test cases. Tool execution 
follows a whitelisting protocol: read-only operations auto-execute, while state-changing actions require explicit human 
conﬁrmation.
5. Synchronization Layer as consistency guarantee
The Synchronization Layer simultaneously functions as a validation layer: changes are not processed in isolation but 
reconciled system-wide. Inconsistencies between touchpoints are detected and resolved before they produce 
operational errors.
6. EU-sovereign infrastructure as trust foundation
The entire platform runs on IONOS EU cloud with GDPR-compliant data processing. The architecture addresses EU AI 
Act requirements regarding risk assessment and documentation capability. Data sovereignty, traceability, and 
governance are architecturally embedded - not as a retrospective compliance exercise, but as an integral part of 
system design.
7. Internal benchmarks and iterative optimization
KPI measurements for administration reduction, process costs, and participant satisfaction are collected on a project-
speciﬁc basis and feed into continuous improvement of AI models, assistants, and agents. The Learn Machine closes 
the feedback loop: insights from every event improve quality for the next.

Questions / Answers
What type of AI model or technique does your solution use? Please provide a simple, high-level explanation that
helps judges understand the approach (no conﬁdential or proprietary details required).
eBrain employs a multi-layer AI architecture connecting three technology levels - each optimized for diﬀerent 
operational requirements in attendee management.
Foundation models and provider architecture:
The primary LLM is GPT-4.1, accessed via Azure OpenAI, delivering high-performance reasoning for content 
generation, natural-language query processing, and agentic tool orchestration. For resilience and EU data sovereignty, 
an automatic fallback routes to Meta Llama 3.3-70B-Instruct hosted on IONOS EU cloud - ensuring uninterrupted 
operation even when primary provider content ﬁlters trigger on edge-case inputs. Image generation uses Black Forest 
Labs FLUX.1-schnell, also hosted on IONOS, for AI-powered event visuals and email headers. This dual-provider 
architecture guarantees both performance and EU-sovereign data processing.
Agentic architecture with function calling:
Emily operates through an agentic loop - the LLM reasons about user intent, selects from tool functions across both 
the Python AI layer and the Ruby backend, executes them, evaluates results, and chains further actions - up to ten 
iterations per interaction. Safe operations auto-execute via whitelisting; state-changing actions require explicit user 
conﬁrmation.
Specialized AI assistants:
Along every phase of the event lifecycle - Setup, Run, Onsite, Learn - contextually embedded assistants operate. 
These are conﬁgured for speciﬁc operational tasks: website building assistance, communication creation, reporting 
interpretation, operational questions. They deliver contextual guidance adapted to the current data situation, event 
status, and user role.
Task-oriented AI agents:
Agents take over or support concrete operational steps. The CTA agent suggests daily prioritized to-dos based on 
current event status. Additional agents support email creation, data reconciliation, status updates, and action 
recommendations. Agents operate in a suggestive mode — ﬁnal decisions remain with the human operator.
NLP for feedback analysis:
Natural Language Processing is deployed in the Learn Machine to analyze free-text feedback from surveys and 
participant comments - pattern recognition, sentiment analysis, and automated summaries enable insights that would 
take days manually.
Emily as the integrating dialog layer:
Emily is the user-facing interface connecting all three AI levels. She processes natural-language queries, routes them to 
the appropriate subsystem - LLM, assistant, or agent - and delivers answers, recommendations, or action suggestions 
in a uniﬁed dialogic interaction.
Technical stack and infrastructure:
Python FastAPI microservices for AI processing, Rails 8 backend for data persistence and tool execution, Nuxt 3 and 
Vue frontend, PostgreSQL for operational and AI session data. YAML-based prompt engineering with versioned 
templates ensures reproducible AI behavior. The entire architecture runs on EU-sovereign cloud infrastructure (IONOS), 
is GDPR-compliant, and addresses EU AI Act documentation requirements.
